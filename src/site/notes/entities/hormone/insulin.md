<h1>Insulin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Insulin is a 51-amino-acid peptide hormone produced by the beta cells of the pancreatic islets of Langerhans. It is the primary anabolic hormone of the body, promoting glucose uptake into muscle and adipose tissue, glycogen synthesis, lipogenesis, and protein synthesis while inhibiting gluconeogenesis, lipolysis, and proteolysis. Insulin signaling through the PI3K/Akt pathway is one of the most studied signaling cascades in biology. Insulin resistance — where cells fail to respond normally to insulin — is the unifying pathology behind metabolic syndrome, type 2 diabetes, NAFLD, PCOS, and is increasingly linked to Alzheimer's disease (&quot;type 3 diabetes&quot;).</p>
<h2>Key Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Glucose uptake via GLUT4 translocation (muscle, adipose)</li>
<li>Glycogen synthesis (liver, muscle)</li>
<li>Lipogenesis and inhibition of lipolysis</li>
<li>Protein synthesis (mTOR activation)</li>
<li>Suppression of hepatic glucose output</li>
<li>CNS effects: appetite regulation, memory, neuroprotection</li>
</ul>
<h2>Regulation</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Stimulated by</strong>: Glucose, amino acids (leucine), GLP-1, GIP, vagal stimulation</li>
<li><strong>Inhibited by</strong>: Somatostatin, epinephrine, low glucose, fasting</li>
<li><strong>Circadian pattern</strong>: Higher sensitivity in morning, lower at night</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Relevance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Type 2 diabetes</strong> — Progressive insulin resistance and beta cell failure</li>
<li><strong>Type 1 diabetes</strong> — Autoimmune destruction of beta cells (absolute deficiency)</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic syndrome</strong> — Hyperinsulinemia drives hypertension, dyslipidemia, visceral obesity</li>
<li><strong>PCOS</strong> — Insulin resistance drives androgen excess</li>
<li><strong>Alzheimer's disease</strong> — Brain insulin resistance (&quot;type 3 diabetes&quot; hypothesis)</li>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong> — Hyperinsulinemia promotes cell proliferation via IGF-1 axis</li>
</ul>
