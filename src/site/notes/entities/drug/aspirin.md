<h1>Aspirin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid) is one of the most widely used medications in history, first synthesized by Bayer in 1897. It is unique among NSAIDs because it <strong>irreversibly</strong> acetylates cyclooxygenase enzymes, providing a permanent antiplatelet effect that lasts the entire 7-10 day lifespan of platelets. This mechanism underlies its use in cardiovascular prevention. At low doses (75-100 mg), aspirin primarily inhibits platelet COX-1 (reducing thromboxane A2 and platelet aggregation). At higher doses (650-1000 mg), it provides anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects through broader COX-1/COX-2 inhibition.</p>
<h2>Dosing</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular prevention</strong>: 75-100 mg daily (low-dose)</li>
<li><strong>Pain/fever</strong>: 325-650 mg every 4-6 hours</li>
<li><strong>Anti-inflammatory</strong>: 650-1000 mg every 4-6 hours (max 4g/day)</li>
<li><strong>Acute MI</strong>: 325 mg chewed immediately (fastest absorption)</li>
</ul>
