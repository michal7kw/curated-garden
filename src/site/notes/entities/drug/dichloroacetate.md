<h1>Dichloroacetate (DCA)</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Dichloroacetate is a pyruvate dehydrogenase kinase (PDK) inhibitor that activates the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/pyruvate-dehydrogenase/" class="internal-link">Pyruvate Dehydrogenase</a> complex, promoting oxidative metabolism over glycolysis. It has clinical applications in lactic acidosis and is being investigated for cancer metabolism modulation.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Target</strong>: Pyruvate dehydrogenase kinase (PDK1-4)</li>
<li><strong>Effect</strong>: Inhibits PDK → keeps <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/pyruvate-dehydrogenase/" class="internal-link">Pyruvate Dehydrogenase</a> in active (dephosphorylated) state</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic shift</strong>: Pyruvate → <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/acetyl-coa/" class="internal-link">Acetyl-CoA</a> (via PDH) instead of pyruvate → <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/lactate/" class="internal-link">Lactate</a> (via LDH)</li>
<li><strong>Net effect</strong>: Increased mitochondrial <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/citric-acid-cycle/" class="internal-link">Citric Acid Cycle</a> flux and <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/oxidative-phosphorylation/" class="internal-link">Oxidative Phosphorylation</a>, decreased glycolytic lactate production</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Applications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lactic acidosis</strong>: Reduces blood lactate by promoting pyruvate oxidation through PDH</li>
<li><strong>Cancer research</strong>: Cancer cells often exhibit the Warburg effect (aerobic glycolysis); DCA may reverse this by reactivating PDH and mitochondrial oxidation</li>
<li><strong>Mitochondrial diseases</strong>: Potential benefit in PDH dysfunction by maximizing residual enzyme activity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Precautions</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Peripheral neuropathy</strong>: Dose-limiting toxicity (reversible)</li>
<li><strong>Clinical supervision required</strong>: Not an over-the-counter supplement</li>
<li><strong>Variable response</strong>: Effectiveness depends on the specific PDK isoform expression</li>
</ul>
