<h1>Nalidixic Acid</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Nalidixic Acid was the first quinolone antibiotic introduced. It is effective against most <em>E. coli</em>, <em>Enterobacter</em>, and <em>Klebsiella</em> species but has no activity against <em>Pseudomonas</em>. Its use has largely been superseded by newer fluoroquinolones like Ciprofloxacin and Ofloxacin.</p>
<h2>Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Uncomplicated Urinary Tract Infections.</strong></li>
</ul>
