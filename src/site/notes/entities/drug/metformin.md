<h1>METFORMIN</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>First-line biguanide antidiabetic drug that activates AMPK, inhibits mitochondrial complex I, and reduces hepatic glucose production. Under investigation as a longevity agent in the landmark TAME trial. Not metabolized by CYP enzymes; eliminated renally via OCT2/MATE transporters.</p>
<h2>Pharmacology</h2>
<p><strong>Mechanism of Action:</strong><br />
Metformin's primary mechanism is inhibition of mitochondrial complex I (NADH:ubiquinone oxidoreductase), which reduces hepatic energy charge, activating AMPK (AMP-activated protein kinase) as the central energy sensor. AMPK activation suppresses hepatic gluconeogenesis through downregulation of transcription factors PEPCK and G6Pase. Secondary effects include improved peripheral insulin sensitivity, decreased intestinal glucose absorption, and altered gut microbiome composition.</p>
<p><strong>Pharmacokinetics:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Absorption: Bioavailability 50-60%, absorbed in the upper small intestine via OCT1 transporter</li>
<li>Distribution: Negligible protein binding; distributes to erythrocytes (12-18h half-life in blood vs 4-9h in plasma)</li>
<li>Metabolism: Not metabolized by CYP enzymes — unique among oral antidiabetics</li>
<li>Elimination: Renal excretion via OCT2 (uptake) and MATE1/MATE2-K (efflux) transporters</li>
<li>Half-life: 4-9 hours (plasma); 12-18 hours (erythrocytes)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Key Drug Interactions:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Contrast dye: Withhold 48h before/after iodinated contrast (acute kidney injury risk)</li>
<li>Alcohol: Increased lactic acidosis risk</li>
<li>Cimetidine: Competes for renal tubular secretion, increases metformin levels</li>
<li>Vitamin B12: Reduced absorption with long-term use (10-30% deficiency rate)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Use</h2>
<p><strong>Primary Indications:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Type 2 diabetes mellitus (first-line therapy per ADA/EASD guidelines)</li>
<li>Prediabetes (off-label, ADA recommends consideration for high-risk patients)</li>
<li>PCOS (off-label, improves insulin resistance and ovulatory function)</li>
<li>Gestational diabetes (off-label, increasingly used as alternative to insulin)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Dosing:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Start: 500 mg once daily with evening meal (to minimize GI effects)</li>
<li>Titrate: Increase by 500 mg weekly to target dose</li>
<li>Target: 1000 mg twice daily (2000 mg/day) — maximum glycemic benefit</li>
<li>Maximum: 2550 mg/day (rarely needed)</li>
<li>Extended-release (XR): Better GI tolerability, once-daily dosing</li>
<li>Renal dose adjustment: eGFR 30-45: reduce to 1000 mg/day; eGFR &lt;30: contraindicated</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Longevity Research:</strong><br />
The TAME (Targeting Aging with Metformin) trial is the first FDA-approved clinical trial targeting aging as an indication. Observational data suggest metformin users have reduced all-cause mortality, cancer incidence, and cognitive decline compared to matched diabetics on other therapies. The proposed longevity mechanisms include AMPK-mediated autophagy, reduced mTOR signaling, decreased cellular senescence, and improved mitochondrial function.</p>
<h2>Contraindications</h2>
<ul>
<li>eGFR &lt;30 mL/min/1.73m² (absolute contraindication)</li>
<li>eGFR 30-45 mL/min/1.73m² (dose reduction required)</li>
<li>Acute or chronic metabolic acidosis including diabetic ketoacidosis</li>
<li>Active liver disease with elevated transaminases</li>
<li>Before/after iodinated contrast procedures (withhold 48h)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Side Effects</h2>
<ul>
<li>Gastrointestinal symptoms (nausea, diarrhea, abdominal pain — most common, usually transient, mitigated by slow titration)</li>
<li>Metallic taste (dose-related)</li>
<li>Vitamin B12 deficiency (10-30% with long-term use, monitor annually)</li>
<li>Lactic acidosis (extremely rare, &lt;10 per 100,000 patient-years; almost always in setting of acute renal failure)</li>
<li>Weight neutral or modest weight loss (advantage over sulfonylureas and insulin)</li>
</ul>
