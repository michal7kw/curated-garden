<h1>Memantine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Memantine (Namenda) represents a distinct class of Alzheimer's treatment. Unlike cholinesterase inhibitors (which boost signal), Memantine reduces <strong>noise</strong> (excitotoxicity).</p>
<p>In Alzheimer's, damaged neurons release excessive <strong>Glutamate</strong>, which overstimulates NMDA receptors, leading to toxic Calcium influx and cell death. Memantine blocks this &quot;tonic&quot; overstimulation without interfering with the &quot;phasic&quot; signals required for learning.</p>
<h2>Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Alzheimer's Disease</strong>: Moderate to Severe (often combined with Donepezil).</li>
<li><strong>Vascular Dementia</strong>: Off-label.</li>
</ul>
