<h1>Fluoxetine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>First widely prescribed SSRI antidepressant (Prozac). Long half-life allows flexible dosing and easier discontinuation. FDA-approved for depression, OCD, panic disorder, bulimia, and PMDD.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Fluoxetine selectively inhibits the serotonin reuptake transporter (SERT) at presynaptic neurons, blocking the reabsorption of serotonin (5-HT) from the synaptic cleft and increasing its availability for postsynaptic receptor binding. The therapeutic delay of 2-4 weeks is attributed to gradual desensitization of somatodendritic 5-HT1A autoreceptors, which initially limit serotonin release in response to reuptake inhibition. Its active metabolite norfluoxetine has a half-life of 4-16 days, providing uniquely prolonged activity among SSRIs. Fluoxetine also inhibits CYP2D6, which accounts for many of its drug-drug interactions.</p>
