<h1>Time-Restricted Eating</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Form of intermittent fasting that restricts daily food intake to a consistent window (typically 8-12 hours), aligning eating with circadian rhythms. Distinguished from general intermittent fasting by emphasis on meal timing rather than caloric restriction. Satchin Panda's research shows benefits for metabolic health independent of caloric intake.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Aligns nutrient intake with circadian clock gene expression (BMAL1, CLOCK, Per, Cry). Extends overnight fasting period, allowing autophagy activation, insulin sensitivity recovery, and circadian metabolic optimization. Reduces late-night eating which disrupts peripheral clocks. Enhances fat oxidation during extended fasting window.</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Window</strong>: 8-12 hours of eating per day</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Ideally start 1-2h after waking, stop 2-3h before sleep</li>
<li><strong>Most studied</strong>: 16:8 (16h fast, 8h eat)</li>
<li><strong>Key principle</strong>: Consistency of timing matters more than exact window size</li>
</ul>
