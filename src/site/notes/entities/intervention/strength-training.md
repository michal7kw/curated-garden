<h1>Strength Training</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Resistance exercise using progressive overload to increase muscular strength, mass, and endurance. Foundational for metabolic health, bone density, functional independence, and longevity. Stimulates muscle protein synthesis, growth hormone and testosterone release, and improves insulin sensitivity. WHO recommends 2+ sessions per week for all adults.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Mechanical tension and metabolic stress on muscle fibers activate mTOR signaling pathway, stimulating muscle protein synthesis. Triggers hormonal responses (GH, testosterone, IGF-1). Increases bone mineral density via mechanical loading (Wolff's law). Enhances glucose disposal via GLUT4 translocation. Preserves lean mass which maintains basal metabolic rate.</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Frequency</strong>: 2-4 sessions per week</li>
<li><strong>Volume</strong>: 8-12 reps per set, 2-4 sets per exercise</li>
<li><strong>Key principle</strong>: Progressive overload (gradually increase resistance)</li>
<li><strong>Recovery</strong>: 48-72 hours between training same muscle group</li>
</ul>
