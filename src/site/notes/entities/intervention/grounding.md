<h1>Grounding</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Practice of direct physical contact with the Earth's surface (barefoot walking, lying on ground) or using conductive systems connected to ground. Proposed mechanism involves transfer of free electrons from Earth's surface, reducing inflammation and normalizing cortisol rhythm. Evidence is preliminary but growing, with small studies showing improved sleep, pain, and inflammatory markers.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Proposed electron transfer from Earth's surface provides antioxidant effects by neutralizing reactive oxygen species. May influence cortisol circadian rhythm, reduce blood viscosity (zeta potential changes), and modulate autonomic nervous system. Mechanism still under investigation — stronger placebo-controlled trials needed.</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Duration</strong>: 30-60 minutes daily</li>
<li><strong>Method</strong>: Barefoot on grass, soil, sand, or concrete</li>
<li><strong>Indoor</strong>: Grounding mats/sheets connected to ground port</li>
<li><strong>Conductive surfaces</strong>: Grass, soil, sand, concrete (NOT asphalt or wood)</li>
</ul>
