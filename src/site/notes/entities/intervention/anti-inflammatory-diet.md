<h1>Anti-Inflammatory Diet</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The anti-inflammatory diet is a nutritional framework designed to combat chronic low-grade inflammation — now recognized as the common denominator in cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, cancer, neurodegeneration, depression, and accelerated aging (&quot;inflammaging&quot;). Rather than a rigid meal plan, it describes principles shared by the world's healthiest dietary patterns (Mediterranean, traditional Japanese, Nordic). The core strategy is to increase anti-inflammatory inputs (omega-3 fatty acids, polyphenols, fiber, probiotics) while eliminating pro-inflammatory triggers (refined carbohydrates, industrial seed oils, processed foods, excess alcohol).</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: dietary</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Increases omega-3/omega-6 ratio (reduces prostaglandin E2 and leukotriene B4), provides polyphenols that inhibit NF-κB and COX-2, feeds anti-inflammatory gut microbiome (butyrate-producing bacteria via fiber), and reduces AGE formation (advanced glycation end-products from processed foods).</p>
<h2>Key Foods</h2>
<h3>Anti-Inflammatory (Emphasize)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Fatty fish</strong> (salmon, sardines, mackerel) — EPA/DHA omega-3s</li>
<li><strong>Extra virgin olive oil</strong> — Oleocanthal (natural COX inhibitor)</li>
<li><strong>Berries</strong> (blueberries, strawberries) — Anthocyanins</li>
<li><strong>Leafy greens</strong> (spinach, kale) — Folate, carotenoids</li>
<li><strong>Turmeric/ginger</strong> — Curcumin, gingerols</li>
<li><strong>Nuts</strong> (walnuts, almonds) — ALA omega-3, polyphenols</li>
<li><strong>Fermented foods</strong> (sauerkraut, kimchi, yogurt) — Probiotics</li>
</ul>
<h3>Pro-Inflammatory (Minimize)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Refined sugar and high-fructose corn syrup</li>
<li>Industrial seed oils (soybean, corn, sunflower — high omega-6)</li>
<li>Processed meats (nitrates, AGEs)</li>
<li>Ultra-processed foods (emulsifiers disrupt gut barrier)</li>
<li>Excess alcohol (&gt;1 drink/day)</li>
<li>Trans fats</li>
</ul>
