<h1>Breathwork</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Deliberate control of breathing patterns to influence autonomic nervous system function, stress response, and cognitive state. Encompasses slow breathing (4-6 breaths/min for parasympathetic activation), cyclic hyperventilation (Wim Hof, Tummo), box breathing, and physiological sighs. Evidence strongest for stress reduction and autonomic regulation.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Slow breathing activates vagal tone and parasympathetic nervous system via baroreceptor stimulation. Cyclic hyperventilation induces transient respiratory alkalosis, catecholamine release, and temporary immune modulation. Physiological sighs (double inhale + long exhale) re-inflate alveoli and rapidly reduce autonomic arousal.</p>
<h2>Protocols</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Slow breathing</strong>: 4-6 breaths/min, 5-20 min (parasympathetic activation)</li>
<li><strong>Box breathing</strong>: 4-4-4-4 count (military stress management)</li>
<li><strong>Cyclic hyperventilation</strong>: 30 breaths + breath hold (Wim Hof method)</li>
<li><strong>Physiological sigh</strong>: Double inhale through nose + long exhale through mouth (acute stress relief)</li>
</ul>
