<h1>Spermidine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p><strong>Spermidine</strong> is a naturally occurring polyamine found in all living cells, where it plays essential roles in cell growth, gene expression, and protein synthesis. It belongs to the polyamine family (putrescine → spermidine → spermine) and is one of the most potent natural inducers of <strong>autophagy</strong> — the cellular recycling process that degrades damaged organelles and misfolded proteins.</p>
<p>Dietary spermidine intake declines with age (as does endogenous synthesis), and epidemiological data from the <strong>Bruneck Study</strong> (20-year follow-up) associates high spermidine intake with significantly reduced cardiovascular and all-cause mortality — a 5.7-year life expectancy advantage in the highest vs lowest tertile.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Autophagy Induction (Primary)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>EP300 (p300) acetyltransferase inhibition</strong>: spermidine is a competitive inhibitor of EP300, which acetylates autophagy proteins (ATG5, ATG7, ATG12, Beclin-1, LC3). Inhibition → hypoacetylation → autophagy activation</li>
<li><strong>AMPK activation</strong>: spermidine activates AMPK → inhibits mTORC1 → releases autophagy suppression</li>
<li><strong>TFEB nuclear translocation</strong>: promotes TFEB (transcription factor EB) entry into the nucleus → upregulates lysosome biogenesis and autophagy genes</li>
<li><strong>Mitophagy</strong>: activates PINK1/Parkin pathway → selective degradation of damaged mitochondria</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cardioprotection</h3>
<ul>
<li>Improves mitochondrial function in cardiomyocytes</li>
<li>Reduces cardiac hypertrophy and arterial stiffness in aged mice</li>
<li>Enhances diastolic function via titin phosphorylation</li>
<li>Anti-inflammatory: reduces NLRP3 inflammasome activation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Neuroprotection</h3>
<ul>
<li>Clears protein aggregates (α-synuclein, tau, polyglutamine) via autophagy</li>
<li>Maintains synaptic vesicle pool in aged neurons</li>
<li>Preserves hippocampal neurogenesis</li>
<li>SmartAge trial: improved memory performance in older adults at risk for dementia</li>
</ul>
<h3>Epigenetic Regulation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Spermidine is substrate for eIF5A hypusination — essential for translation elongation of polyproline-containing proteins</li>
<li>Modifies chromatin structure (polyamines bind DNA and alter nucleosome positioning)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Evidence</h2>
<h3>Lifespan Extension</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Eisenberg et al. 2009</strong> (PMID: 19801973): spermidine extended lifespan in yeast, flies, worms, and human immune cells. Mechanism: autophagy-dependent.</li>
<li><strong>Eisenberg et al. 2016</strong> (PMID: 27841876): oral spermidine extended lifespan and reduced cardiac aging in mice. Cardioprotection via enhanced mitophagy and mitochondrial function.</li>
<li><strong>Bruneck Study</strong> (Kiechl et al. 2018, PMID: 30094658): prospective cohort (N=829), 20-year follow-up. Highest tertile of dietary spermidine intake: HR 0.62 for all-cause mortality. 5.7 years additional life expectancy.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Human Clinical Trials</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>SmartAge Trial</strong> (PMID: 30364145): 12-month RCT in older adults with subjective cognitive decline. Spermidine-rich wheat germ extract improved memory performance (mnemonic discrimination).</li>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular</strong>: ongoing Phase 2 trial of spermidine for diastolic heart failure (HFpEF)</li>
<li><strong>COVID-19</strong>: spermidine shown to inhibit SARS-CoV-2 replication in vitro via autophagy enhancement</li>
</ul>
