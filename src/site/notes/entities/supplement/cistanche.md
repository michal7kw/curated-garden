<h1>Cistanche</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Parasitic desert plant (Cistanche tubulosa/deserticola) used in Traditional Chinese Medicine for over 1,800 years as a kidney and yang tonic. Contains phenylethanoid glycosides (echinacoside, acteoside) with demonstrated neuroprotective, anti-inflammatory, and testosterone-supporting properties. Known as &quot;Ginseng of the Desert.&quot; Evidence is primarily preclinical with limited human trials.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Echinacoside and acteoside provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Increases nerve growth factor (NGF) expression supporting neurogenesis. Modulates testosterone via HPG axis stimulation. Inhibits acetylcholinesterase enhancing cholinergic signaling. Activates Nrf2 antioxidant pathway.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Standardized extract</strong>: 100-300mg daily (15-30% echinacoside)</li>
<li><strong>Traditional</strong>: Hot water decoction</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: With food for enhanced absorption</li>
</ul>
