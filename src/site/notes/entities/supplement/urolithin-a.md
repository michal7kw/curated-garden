<h1>Urolithin A</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p><strong>Urolithin A (UA)</strong> is a metabolite produced by gut bacteria from ellagitannins and ellagic acid, found in pomegranates, walnuts, raspberries, and strawberries. It is the <strong>first compound identified to specifically induce mitophagy</strong> (selective autophagy of damaged mitochondria) in a clinically meaningful way.</p>
<p>A critical challenge: only approximately <strong>40% of people</strong> have the gut microbiome composition needed to convert dietary ellagitannins into urolithin A (requiring specific Gordonibacter and Ellagibacter species). Direct supplementation with synthetic urolithin A bypasses this bottleneck, providing consistent bioactive levels regardless of microbiome composition.</p>
<p>Developed by <strong>Amazentis</strong> (spin-off from EPFL, Switzerland), urolithin A is marketed as <strong>Mitopure</strong> (Timeline Nutrition) and has received <strong>FDA GRAS status</strong> (2022).</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Mitophagy Activation (Primary)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>PINK1/Parkin pathway</strong>: damaged mitochondria lose membrane potential → PINK1 accumulates on outer membrane → recruits Parkin E3 ligase → ubiquitination of outer membrane proteins → autophagosome engulfment → lysosomal degradation</li>
<li>Urolithin A enhances this pathway at multiple points: stabilizes PINK1, increases Parkin recruitment, upregulates autophagy receptor proteins (BNIP3, NIX)</li>
<li>Result: selective clearance of dysfunctional mitochondria, leaving healthy mitochondria intact</li>
</ul>
<h3>Mitochondrial Biogenesis</h3>
<ul>
<li>Upregulates <strong>PGC-1α</strong> (master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis)</li>
<li>Increases mitochondrial DNA copy number and respiratory chain complex expression</li>
<li>Net effect: replacement of damaged mitochondria with healthy new ones → improved cellular energetics</li>
</ul>
<h3>Anti-inflammatory</h3>
<ul>
<li>Inhibits <strong>NLRP3 inflammasome</strong> activation → reduces IL-1β and IL-18</li>
<li>Suppresses NF-κB signaling → reduces TNF-α, IL-6</li>
<li>Particularly effective against age-related sterile inflammation (inflammaging)</li>
</ul>
<h3>NAD+ Metabolism</h3>
<ul>
<li>Enhances NAD+ biosynthesis → supports sirtuin activity and mitochondrial function</li>
<li>Complementary to NMN/NR supplementation approaches</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Evidence</h2>
<h3>Preclinical</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Ryu et al. 2016</strong> (PMID: 27127839): UA extends lifespan in C. elegans by 45%, requires PINK1-dependent mitophagy. In mice, UA improved muscle function in aged animals — increased running endurance, improved grip strength.</li>
<li><strong>D'Amico et al. 2021</strong> (PMID: 33972840): UA reduced neuroinflammation and improved cognitive function in Alzheimer's mouse model (APP/PS1). Cleared dysfunctional mitochondria in neurons.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Human Clinical Trials</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>First-in-human safety</strong> (Andreux et al. 2019, PMID: 30989130): 500, 1000, 2000mg single-dose and 28-day multiple-dose in healthy sedentary elderly. Well-tolerated at all doses. Dose-dependent improvement in mitochondrial biomarkers (acylcarnitine profile, muscle gene expression).</li>
<li><strong>ATLAS Trial</strong> (Singh et al. 2022, PMID: 35550556): Phase 2 RCT, 88 adults aged 65-90. 1000mg UA daily for 4 months → significant improvement in muscle endurance (6-minute walk distance), reduced inflammatory biomarkers (CRP, acylcarnitines), improved mitochondrial gene expression in skeletal muscle biopsies.</li>
<li><strong>NOURISH Study</strong>: confirmed bioavailability and safety in larger cohort. Muscle function improvements in 2-month and 4-month timepoints.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Microbiome Connection</h3>
<ul>
<li>Ellagitannin → ellagic acid → urolithin A conversion requires <strong>Gordonibacter urolithinfaciens</strong> and <strong>Ellagibacter isourolithinifaciens</strong></li>
<li>&quot;UA producer&quot; phenotype: ~40% of Western adults, higher in Mediterranean diet adherents</li>
<li>Direct UA supplementation provides 6x higher plasma levels than pomegranate juice</li>
</ul>
