<h1>GABA</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Primary inhibitory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system. Counterbalances excitatory glutamate signaling, essential for preventing neural overexcitation.</p>
<p><strong>Chemical Formula:</strong> C4H9NO2<br />
<strong>Molecular Weight:</strong> 103.12</p>
<h2>Primary Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Neural inhibition</li>
<li>Anxiety reduction</li>
<li>Muscle relaxation</li>
<li>Sleep promotion</li>
<li>Seizure prevention</li>
</ul>
<h2>Receptor Types</h2>
<ul>
<li>GABA-A (fast inhibitory, ionotropic, Cl⁻ channel)</li>
<li>GABA-B (slow inhibitory, metabotropic, K⁺/Ca²⁺)</li>
<li>GABA-C (retinal, ionotropic)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Synthesis Pathway</h2>
<p>Glutamate → GABA (glutamic acid decarboxylase / GAD)</p>
<h2>Associated Conditions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Anxiety disorders (GABAergic deficit)</li>
<li>Epilepsy (excitatory/inhibitory imbalance)</li>
<li>Insomnia (reduced GABAergic tone)</li>
<li>Huntington's disease (GABAergic neuron loss)</li>
<li>Alcohol dependence (GABA-A receptor modulation)</li>
</ul>
