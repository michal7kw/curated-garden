<h1>Tongkat Ali</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia Jack) is a medicinal plant native to Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Indonesia, Vietnam) whose root has been used traditionally as an aphrodisiac, energy tonic, antimalarial, and febrifuge. The bioactive constituents are primarily quassinoids (eurycomanone, eurycolactone, eurycomalactone), along with canthin-6-one alkaloids and beta-carboline alkaloids. Eurycomanone is considered the primary marker compound for standardization and quality control.</p>
<p>A 2022 meta-analysis of 5 RCTs confirmed statistically significant increases in total testosterone (SMD 1.352, p = 0.001), with particularly strong effects in hypogonadal men. A 4-week RCT demonstrated a 16% reduction in salivary cortisol alongside a 37% increase in salivary testosterone, supporting its traditional use as an adaptogen. Evidence for fertility benefits includes a 44% improvement in sperm motility in one 12-week RCT. Tongkat Ali is among the better-studied testosterone-supporting supplements, though effect sizes remain modest compared to pharmaceutical interventions.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 3/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Tongkat Ali exerts its effects through several interconnected mechanisms:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>SHBG reduction:</strong> Lowers sex hormone-binding globulin levels, increasing the fraction of free (bioavailable) testosterone. This is the primary mechanism supported by clinical data.</li>
<li><strong>HPA axis modulation:</strong> Reduces cortisol output via regulation of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis, improving the cortisol-to-testosterone ratio. Demonstrated in RCT data showing -16% cortisol and +37% testosterone.</li>
<li><strong>Aromatase inhibition:</strong> Eurycomanone inhibits CYP19 (aromatase enzyme), reducing conversion of testosterone to estradiol. This effect is modest compared to pharmaceutical aromatase inhibitors.</li>
<li><strong>Leydig cell activation:</strong> Stimulates Leydig cell steroidogenesis via the cAMP-PKA signaling pathway, enhancing endogenous testosterone production.</li>
<li><strong>Phosphodiesterase inhibition:</strong> Some quassinoids inhibit PDE enzymes, potentially contributing to ergogenic effects and improved blood flow.</li>
<li><strong>Dopamine modulation:</strong> Preclinical evidence suggests interaction with dopaminergic pathways, which may contribute to mood enhancement and libido effects.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Timing</h3>
<ul>
<li>Take in the morning or early afternoon with food</li>
<li>Avoid taking after 3 PM (may cause insomnia due to stimulating effects)</li>
<li>Cycling: 4 weeks on, 1-2 weeks off is commonly recommended, though not formally studied</li>
<li>May be stacked with ashwagandha for combined adaptogenic effects</li>
</ul>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>Hormone-sensitive cancers (prostate, breast) — androgenic effects may stimulate tumor growth</li>
<li>Pregnancy and breastfeeding — insufficient safety data</li>
<li>Prostate hypertrophy — monitor PSA regularly if using</li>
<li>Children and adolescents — may affect hormonal development</li>
</ul>
<h3>Drug Interactions</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Anticoagulants:</strong> Mild antiplatelet effects reported; monitor INR</li>
<li><strong>Antihypertensives:</strong> Additive blood pressure reduction possible</li>
<li><strong>Hormone therapies (TRT, SERMs):</strong> Additive androgenic effects</li>
<li><strong>Immunosuppressants:</strong> Immune-stimulating properties may counteract</li>
</ul>
<h3>Side Effects</h3>
<p>Well-tolerated at standard doses (200-400 mg/day). Higher doses may cause restlessness, insomnia, and irritability. Mild GI discomfort occurs rarely. Oral LD50 of aqueous extract exceeds 3,000 mg/kg in mice, indicating a wide therapeutic margin.</p>
<h3>Monitoring</h3>
<ul>
<li>Monitor total and free testosterone, SHBG, and estradiol at baseline and periodically</li>
<li>PSA monitoring recommended for men over 40</li>
<li>Cortisol levels may be informative for stress-focused use</li>
</ul>
<h2>Quality Considerations</h2>
<ul>
<li>Standardization to eurycomanone content (1-2%) is critical for reproducible effects</li>
<li>LJ100 and Physta are the most clinically validated extracts</li>
<li>Ethanol extracts contain different bioactive profiles than water extracts — prefer water extracts which match the clinical evidence</li>
<li>Adulteration with sildenafil analogs has been reported in some commercial products — choose reputable brands with third-party testing</li>
<li>Root source matters: Indonesian and Malaysian roots have the highest eurycomanone content</li>
</ul>
