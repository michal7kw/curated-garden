<h1>Fisetin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p><strong>Fisetin</strong> (3,3',4',7-tetrahydroxyflavone) is a naturally occurring flavonol found in strawberries (160 μg/g), apples, persimmons, onions, and cucumbers. It was identified as the <strong>most potent natural senolytic</strong> in a Mayo Clinic screening of 10 flavonoids (Yousefzadeh et al., 2018), with the ability to selectively kill senescent cells while sparing normal cells.</p>
<p>Beyond senolytic activity, fisetin demonstrates neuroprotective, anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer, and antioxidant properties, making it one of the most multi-functional polyphenols under active investigation for aging and neurodegeneration.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Senolytic Activity</h3>
<p>Fisetin targets multiple pro-survival pathways that senescent cells depend on for resistance to apoptosis:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway inhibition</strong>: senescent cells rely on hyperactive PI3K/AKT signaling for survival. Fisetin suppresses AKT phosphorylation → promotes apoptosis</li>
<li><strong>BCL-2/BCL-xL inhibition</strong>: acts as a natural BH3 mimetic — reduces anti-apoptotic BCL-2 family protein expression in senescent cells</li>
<li><strong>SASP suppression</strong>: inhibits NF-κB → reduces production of inflammatory SASP factors (IL-6, IL-8, MCP-1, MMP3)</li>
<li><strong>HIF-1α inhibition</strong>: blocks hypoxic survival signaling in senescent cells</li>
</ul>
<h3>Neuroprotective Activity</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>BDNF induction</strong>: upregulates Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor via ERK/CREB pathway</li>
<li><strong>Anti-neuroinflammation</strong>: inhibits microglial activation and pro-inflammatory cytokine release</li>
<li><strong>Amyloid-β reduction</strong>: reduces Aβ production and aggregation in AD models</li>
<li><strong>GSK-3β inhibition</strong>: reduces tau phosphorylation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Antioxidant / Anti-inflammatory</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Nrf2 activation</strong>: induces phase II detoxification enzymes (HO-1, NQO1, SOD)</li>
<li><strong>NF-κB inhibition</strong>: reduces COX-2, iNOS, and inflammatory cytokine expression</li>
<li><strong>Direct ROS scavenging</strong>: catechol B-ring structure provides radical scavenging</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Evidence</h2>
<h3>Preclinical (Strong)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Yousefzadeh et al. 2018</strong> (PMID: 30279143): Fisetin most potent of 10 flavonoids tested for senolytic activity. Late-life fisetin treatment (60mg/kg × 5 days) in 85-week-old mice extended median lifespan by ~10%, reduced senescence markers, improved tissue function.</li>
<li><strong>Neuroprotection</strong>: reduced cognitive decline in multiple AD mouse models (3xTg-AD, APP/PS1). Improved spatial memory, reduced Aβ plaques, decreased neuroinflammation.</li>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong>: anti-proliferative in prostate, breast, lung, colon cancer cell lines (IC50 10-50 μM).</li>
</ul>
<h3>Clinical (Emerging)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>AFFIRM-LITE (NCT03675724)</strong>: Phase 2 for frailty in elderly — interim results suggest reduction in inflammatory markers</li>
<li><strong>COVID-19 (NCT04476159)</strong>: fisetin for COVID-related inflammation in nursing home residents</li>
<li><strong>Osteoarthritis</strong>: ongoing trial for senescent cell clearance in knee OA</li>
<li><strong>Limitation</strong>: low oral bioavailability (~44% in mouse with Tween formulation, likely lower in humans) — liposomal formulations being developed</li>
</ul>
<h3>Dosing Paradigms</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Daily neuroprotective</strong>: 100-500 mg/day (lower doses, continuous)</li>
<li><strong>Intermittent senolytic</strong>: 20 mg/kg body weight × 2 consecutive days, repeated monthly (hit-and-run approach)</li>
<li>The senolytic approach mirrors dasatinib+quercetin dosing — brief exposure kills senescent cells, then withdrawal allows normal cell recovery</li>
</ul>
