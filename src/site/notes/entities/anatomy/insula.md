<h1>Insula</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>The insula (Island of Reil) is a cortical structure buried deep within the lateral sulcus, hidden beneath the frontal, parietal, and temporal opercula. It serves as the brain's primary interoceptive cortex — monitoring internal body states (heartbeat, breathing, gut sensations) and integrating them with emotional processing. The anterior insula is a key hub of the salience network, helping determine what internal and external stimuli deserve attention. It is essential for subjective feeling states, empathy, and the experience of disgust, pain, and cravings.</p>
<h3>Subregions</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Anterior insula</strong> — Conscious awareness of body states, emotional feelings, empathy, social emotions</li>
<li><strong>Posterior insula</strong> — Primary interoceptive cortex, pain processing, temperature, sensorimotor integration</li>
<li><strong>Mid-insula</strong> — Integration zone bridging interoception and cognition</li>
</ul>
