<h1>Raphe Nuclei</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>The raphe nuclei are a cluster of nuclei arranged along the midline of the brainstem, from the midbrain to the medulla. They are the principal source of serotonin (5-HT) in the central nervous system, sending projections to virtually every region of the brain and spinal cord. The dorsal raphe nucleus (DRN), the largest of these nuclei, alone contains approximately 235,000 serotonergic neurons in humans and projects to the cortex, hippocampus, amygdala, basal ganglia, and hypothalamus.</p>
<h3>Key Subdivisions</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dorsal raphe nucleus (DRN)</strong> — Largest serotonin source; projects to forebrain, modulates mood, anxiety, cognition</li>
<li><strong>Median raphe nucleus (MRN)</strong> — Projects to hippocampus and septum; involved in memory and theta rhythm</li>
<li><strong>Raphe magnus</strong> — Pain modulation via descending projections to spinal dorsal horn</li>
<li><strong>Raphe obscurus / pallidus</strong> — Autonomic regulation, thermoregulation, respiratory control</li>
</ul>
