<h1>Global developmental delay</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Global Developmental Delay (GDD) is a profound pediatric phenotype applied to children under the age of five who simultaneously fail to meet anticipated developmental milestones across two or more major domains (gross/fine motor skills, speech/language, cognition, social/personal functioning, and activities of daily living).</p>
<p>Because the pediatric brain is rapidly myelinating and building fundamental neural architectures during these early years, missing these milestones indicates a potentially devastating underlying neurological, genetic, or metabolic catastrophe. It operates clinically as a temporary umbrella term; once the child is older and cognitive testing can be reliably mathematically executed, the diagnosis almost universally transitions into Intellectual Disability.</p>
<h2>Clinical Pathologies</h2>
<p>The etiologies driving this phenotype are immensely broad, requiring aggressive clinical neurology mapping:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Genetic/Chromosomal Arrest:</strong> High-priority sweeps check for Trisomy 21 (Down Syndrome), Fragile X, and Rett Syndrome.</li>
<li><strong>Hypoxic Ischemic Encephalopathy (HIE):</strong> Massive oxygen deprivation occurring explicitly during the birthing process structurally kills the periventricular white matter (Cerebral Palsy), permanently blocking motor pathway maturation.</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic Crises:</strong> Inborn errors of metabolism (like Phenylketonuria) allow toxic metabolites to cross the blood-brain barrier and chemically poison the developing cortex.</li>
</ul>
