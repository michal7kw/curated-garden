<h1>Cell Membrane</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Phospholipid bilayer forming the outer boundary of all cells. Selectively permeable barrier that regulates transport of ions, nutrients, and signaling molecules. Contains embedded proteins for signal transduction, cell adhesion, and molecular transport. Rich in cholesterol for fluidity regulation.</p>
<h2>Structure</h2>
<ul>
<li>Phospholipid bilayer with hydrophilic heads and hydrophobic tails</li>
<li>Integral and peripheral membrane proteins</li>
<li>Cholesterol for fluidity and stability</li>
<li>Glycocalyx (carbohydrate coating) for cell recognition</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Selective permeability and molecular transport (channels, pumps, carriers)</li>
<li>Signal transduction via membrane receptors</li>
<li>Cell-cell recognition and adhesion</li>
<li>Maintenance of electrochemical gradients</li>
<li>Endocytosis and exocytosis</li>
</ul>
