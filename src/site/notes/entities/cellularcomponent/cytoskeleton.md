<h1>Cytoskeleton</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Dynamic network of protein filaments providing structural support, cell shape, and intracellular transport. Composed of three main systems — microfilaments (actin), intermediate filaments, and microtubules (tubulin). Essential for cell division, motility, signaling, and organelle positioning.</p>
<h2>Components</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Microfilaments</strong> (actin, 7nm): Cell shape, motility, cytokinesis</li>
<li><strong>Intermediate filaments</strong> (keratin, vimentin, etc., 10nm): Mechanical strength, nuclear lamina</li>
<li><strong>Microtubules</strong> (tubulin, 25nm): Intracellular transport, mitotic spindle, cilia/flagella</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Maintains cell shape and mechanical integrity</li>
<li>Intracellular transport (motor proteins: kinesin, dynein, myosin)</li>
<li>Cell division (mitotic spindle formation)</li>
<li>Cell motility and migration</li>
<li>Signal transduction scaffolding</li>
</ul>
