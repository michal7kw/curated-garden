<h1>Ribosome</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Macromolecular machine composed of ribosomal RNA and proteins that catalyzes protein synthesis (translation). Eukaryotic ribosomes (80S) consist of a large 60S and small 40S subunit. Found free in the cytoplasm or bound to the endoplasmic reticulum. One of the most abundant cellular structures.</p>
<h2>Structure</h2>
<ul>
<li>Large subunit (60S): 28S, 5.8S, 5S rRNA + ~49 proteins</li>
<li>Small subunit (40S): 18S rRNA + ~33 proteins</li>
<li>A, P, E sites for tRNA binding</li>
<li>Assembled in the nucleolus</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>mRNA decoding and protein synthesis (translation)</li>
<li>Co-translational protein folding</li>
<li>Signal recognition particle (SRP) interaction for ER targeting</li>
<li>Quality control (ribosome-associated quality control, RQC)</li>
</ul>
