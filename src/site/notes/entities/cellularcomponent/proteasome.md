<h1>Proteasome</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Large multi-subunit protease complex responsible for regulated degradation of ubiquitin-tagged proteins. The 26S proteasome (20S core + 19S regulatory cap) is the primary mechanism for protein quality control and turnover. Essential for cell cycle regulation, signal transduction, and immune response (antigen processing).</p>
<h2>Structure</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>20S core particle</strong>: Barrel-shaped, 4 stacked rings (alpha-beta-beta-alpha), proteolytic active sites</li>
<li><strong>19S regulatory particle</strong>: Recognizes ubiquitin tags, unfolds substrates, feeds into 20S core</li>
<li><strong>26S proteasome</strong>: Complete complex (20S + one or two 19S caps)</li>
<li><strong>Immunoproteasome</strong>: Alternative form with modified catalytic subunits for enhanced antigen processing</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Degradation of ubiquitin-tagged misfolded or damaged proteins</li>
<li>Cell cycle regulation (cyclin degradation)</li>
<li>NF-kB activation (IkB degradation)</li>
<li>MHC class I antigen processing (immunoproteasome)</li>
<li>Transcription factor turnover</li>
</ul>
