<h1>Golgi Apparatus</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Membrane-bound organelle responsible for modifying, sorting, and packaging proteins and lipids for secretion or delivery to other organelles. Consists of stacked cisternae (cis, medial, trans) with distinct enzymatic activities. Central hub of the endomembrane secretory pathway.</p>
<h2>Structure</h2>
<ul>
<li>Stacked membrane cisternae (cis → medial → trans)</li>
<li>cis-Golgi network (receives from ER)</li>
<li>trans-Golgi network (sorts to destinations)</li>
<li>Associated vesicles for transport</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Post-translational modification (glycosylation, phosphorylation, sulfation)</li>
<li>Protein sorting and packaging into vesicles</li>
<li>Lipid metabolism and sphingolipid synthesis</li>
<li>Lysosome formation (mannose-6-phosphate tagging)</li>
<li>Secretory pathway regulation</li>
</ul>
