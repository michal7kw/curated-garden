<h1>Apolipoprotein B</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Apolipoprotein B (ApoB) is a critical protein component of lipoproteins that are involved in the development of atherosclerosis. There are two primary forms of ApoB: ApoB-48 (synthesized in the small intestine and found in chylomicrons) and ApoB-100 (synthesized in the liver and found in VLDL, IDL, and LDL). In clinical laboratory testing, &quot;ApoB&quot; typically refers to ApoB-100.</p>
<p>Each atherogenic particle—whether it is a large VLDL particle or a small, dense LDL particle—contains exactly one molecule of ApoB-100. Therefore, measuring the total concentration of ApoB provides a direct count of the total number of atherogenic particles in the circulation. This is increasingly recognized as a more accurate measure of cardiovascular risk than LDL cholesterol (LDL-C) concentration, particularly in individuals with &quot;discordance&quot; (e.g., normal LDL-C but a high number of small, dense LDL particles).</p>

<h2>Testing Information</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Measurement Unit</strong>: milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL) or grams per liter (g/L)</li>
<li><strong>Sample Type</strong>: Blood (Serum or Plasma)</li>
<li><strong>Fasting Required</strong>: Preferred (9-12 hours), though ApoB is less affected by recent meals than triglycerides.</li>
<li><strong>Recommended Test Frequency</strong>: Annually; more frequent when monitoring the efficacy of lipid-lowering interventions.</li>
</ul>
<h2>💊 Supplements That Affect This Biomarker</h2>
<h3>Decreasing ApoB</h3>
<ul>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Niacin</a> (Vitamin B3) - Reduces the liver's production of VLDL, leading to a 15-20% reduction in ApoB (High magnitude, Evidence Level 5).</li>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Red Yeast Rice</a> - Contains natural monacolins (similar to statins) that inhibit cholesterol synthesis (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 5).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/omega-3-fatty-acids/" class="internal-link">Omega-3 Fatty Acids</a> - Modestly reduces VLDL production and enhances clearance of triglyceride-rich particles (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/berberine/" class="internal-link">Berberine</a> - Improves LDL receptor expression and reduces hepatic lipoprotein secretion (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Psyllium Husk</a> (Soluble Fiber) - Binds bile acids in the gut, forcing the liver to clear more cholesterol from the blood to synthesize new bile (Mild magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><strong>Plant Sterols/Stanols</strong> - Compete with cholesterol absorption in the intestine.</li>
</ul>
<h2>🧪 Lab Tests That Measure This Biomarker</h2>
<h3>Direct Tests</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>ApoB Blood Test</strong> - Immunoassay for total serum ApoB-100.</li>
<li><strong>Advanced Lipid Panel (NMR/Ion Mobility)</strong> - Often reports ApoB alongside LDL particle number (LDL-P).</li>
</ul>
<h3>Panel Tests</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular Risk Panel</strong> - Comprehensive assessment including ApoB, Lp(a), and hs-CRP.</li>
</ul>
