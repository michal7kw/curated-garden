<h1>Alanine Transaminase</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Alanine Transaminase (ALT), formerly known as Serum Glutamic Pyruvic Transaminase (SGPT), is an enzyme found predominantly in the liver, with smaller amounts in the kidneys, heart, and muscles. Its primary biological function is to catalyze the transfer of an amino group from alanine to α-ketoglutarate, forming pyruvate and glutamate—a key step in the glucose-alanine cycle and gluconeogenesis.</p>
<p>In clinical practice, ALT is one of the most common and sensitive biomarkers for liver injury. Because ALT is primarily localized within hepatocytes, any damage to the liver cell membrane (due to inflammation, toxins, or ischemia) results in the leakage of ALT into the systemic circulation. While not perfectly specific to the liver (it can rise in severe muscle damage), it is much more specific than Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST) for liver-related issues.</p>

<h2>Testing Information</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Measurement Unit</strong>: Units per Liter (U/L) or International Units per Liter (IU/L)</li>
<li><strong>Sample Type</strong>: Blood (Serum or Plasma)</li>
<li><strong>Fasting Required</strong>: False (though often part of a CMP which requires fasting for glucose/lipids)</li>
<li><strong>Recommended Test Frequency</strong>: Annually as part of routine screening; more frequent if monitoring liver disease or hepatotoxic medications.</li>
</ul>
<h2>💊 Supplements That Affect This Biomarker</h2>
<h3>Reducing ALT (Hepatoprotective)</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/silymarin/milk-thistle/" class="internal-link">Milk Thistle</a> (Silymarin) - Well-documented ability to stabilize hepatocyte membranes and reduce enzyme leakage (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/nac/" class="internal-link">NAC</a> (N-Acetyl Cysteine) - Precursor to glutathione; protects liver from oxidative damage and toxins like acetaminophen (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/curcumin/" class="internal-link">Curcumin</a> - Anti-inflammatory effects can reduce liver inflammation in NAFLD patients (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 3).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/taurine/" class="internal-link">Taurine</a> - May help reduce ALT in chronic hepatitis patients (Mild magnitude, Evidence Level 3).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/alpha-lipoic-acid/" class="internal-link">Alpha Lipoic Acid</a> - Potent antioxidant that supports liver detoxification pathways.</li>
</ul>

</ul>
