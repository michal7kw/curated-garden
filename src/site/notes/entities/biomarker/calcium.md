<h1>Calcium</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Calcium is a vital mineral that plays a fundamental role in nearly every biological process. Approximately 99% of the body's calcium is stored in the bones and teeth as hydroxyapatite, providing structural integrity. The remaining 1% circulates in the blood and extracellular fluid, where it is critical for:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Nerve Transmission:</strong> Acting as a secondary messenger for signal transduction.</li>
<li><strong>Muscle Contraction:</strong> Triggering the interaction between actin and myosin.</li>
<li><strong>Blood Clotting:</strong> Serving as Factor IV in the coagulation cascade.</li>
<li><strong>Hormonal Secretion:</strong> Mediating the release of insulin and other hormones.</li>
</ul>
<p>Serum calcium exists in three forms:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Protein-bound (40-45%):</strong> Primarily bound to <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/labtest/albumin/" class="internal-link">Albumin</a>.</li>
<li><strong>Complexed (5-10%):</strong> Bound to anions like citrate or phosphate.</li>
<li><strong>Ionized (50%):</strong> The physiologically active &quot;free&quot; form.</li>
</ol>
<p>Because a large portion of calcium is bound to albumin, &quot;Total Calcium&quot; levels can be misleading if albumin is low. In such cases, &quot;Corrected Calcium&quot; must be calculated or &quot;Ionized Calcium&quot; measured directly.</p>

<h2>Testing Information</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Measurement Unit</strong>: milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL) or millimoles per liter (mmol/L)</li>
<li><strong>Sample Type</strong>: Blood (Serum)</li>
<li><strong>Fasting Required</strong>: False (though often part of a fasting CMP)</li>
<li><strong>Recommended Test Frequency</strong>: Annually; more frequent if monitoring bone disease, kidney stones, or thyroid/parathyroid issues.</li>
</ul>
<h2>💊 Supplements That Affect This Biomarker</h2>
<h3>Increasing Calcium/Absorption</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/vitamin-d/" class="internal-link">Vitamin D</a> - Essential for intestinal calcium absorption (High magnitude, Evidence Level 5).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/labtest/calcium/" class="internal-link">Calcium</a> Supplements - Direct increase in circulating and stored calcium (High magnitude, Evidence Level 5).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/magnesium/" class="internal-link">Magnesium</a> - Acts as a cofactor; deficiency can impair PTH secretion and lead to secondary hypocalcemia (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
</ul>
<h3>Regulating Calcium Distribution</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/vitamin-k2/" class="internal-link">Vitamin K2</a> - Does not necessarily change serum calcium levels but activates osteocalcin to direct calcium into the bone matrix and away from arterial walls (Evidence Level 4).</li>
</ul>
<h2>🧪 Lab Tests That Measure This Biomarker</h2>
<h3>Direct Tests</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Calcium, Total</strong> - Measures all three forms in the blood.</li>
<li><strong>Calcium, Ionized</strong> - The gold standard for assessing physiological activity.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Panel Tests</h3>
<ul>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)</a> - Standard screening.</li>
<li><strong>Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP)</strong> - Often includes total calcium.</li>
<li><strong>Bone Turnover Markers</strong> - Used alongside calcium to assess bone health.</li>
</ul>
