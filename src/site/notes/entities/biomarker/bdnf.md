<h1>BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor)</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF) is a member of the neurotrophin family of growth factors. It plays a critical role in neuronal survival, differentiation, synaptic plasticity, and long-term potentiation (LTP) - the cellular basis of learning and memory. BDNF is synthesized primarily in the brain but also detected in peripheral blood.</p>
<h2>Biological Functions</h2>
<h3>Neuroprotection</h3>
<ul>
<li>Promotes survival of existing neurons</li>
<li>Supports differentiation of new neurons (neurogenesis)</li>
<li>Protects against excitotoxicity and oxidative stress</li>
</ul>
<h3>Synaptic Plasticity</h3>
<ul>
<li>Essential for long-term potentiation (LTP)</li>
<li>Facilitates dendritic spine growth</li>
<li>Modulates neurotransmitter release</li>
<li>Required for memory consolidation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cognitive Function</h3>
<ul>
<li>Hippocampal BDNF critical for memory</li>
<li>Prefrontal BDNF supports executive function</li>
<li>Decline associated with cognitive aging</li>
</ul>

<h3>Factors Affecting Levels</h3>
<p><strong>Increase BDNF:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Aerobic exercise (robust effect)</li>
<li>Cognitive stimulation</li>
<li>Sunlight exposure</li>
<li>Omega-3 fatty acids</li>
<li>Ketogenic diet</li>
<li>Sleep</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Decrease BDNF:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Chronic stress</li>
<li>Depression</li>
<li>Aging</li>
<li>Obesity</li>
<li>Sedentary lifestyle</li>
<li>Poor sleep</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Associations</h2>
<h3>Low BDNF Associated With</h3>
<ul>
<li>Major depressive disorder</li>
<li>Alzheimer's disease</li>
<li>Parkinson's disease</li>
<li>Schizophrenia</li>
<li>Bipolar disorder</li>
<li>Eating disorders</li>
</ul>
<h3>BDNF Val66Met Polymorphism</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Val/Val</strong>: Normal BDNF secretion</li>
<li><strong>Val/Met or Met/Met</strong>: Reduced activity-dependent secretion</li>
<li>Associated with reduced hippocampal volume</li>
<li>May affect antidepressant response</li>
</ul>

<h2>Clinical Interpretation</h2>
<h3>When to Consider</h3>
<ol>
<li>Research context (neuroplasticity studies)</li>
<li>Treatment response monitoring (exercise, antidepressants)</li>
<li>Risk assessment for cognitive decline</li>
</ol>
<h3>Limitations</h3>
<ul>
<li>High inter-individual variability</li>
<li>Platelet BDNF complicates serum interpretation</li>
<li>No established clinical cutoffs</li>
<li>Exercise timing affects levels (acute spike)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Strategies to Optimize BDNF</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Regular aerobic exercise</strong>: 30-45 min, 3-5x/week</li>
<li><strong>Quality sleep</strong>: 7-9 hours, consistent schedule</li>
<li><strong>Stress management</strong>: Meditation, nature exposure</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive challenge</strong>: Learning, mental engagement</li>
<li><strong>Nutrition</strong>: Omega-3s, polyphenols, avoid processed foods</li>
<li><strong>Sunlight</strong>: Morning light exposure</li>
</ol>
