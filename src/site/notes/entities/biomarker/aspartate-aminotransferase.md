<h1>Aspartate Aminotransferase</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST), formerly known as Serum Glutamic Oxaloacetic Transaminase (SGOT), is an enzyme that catalyzes the reversible transfer of an α-amino group between aspartate and glutamate. It is a critical enzyme in the malate-aspartate shuttle and amino acid metabolism.</p>
<p>Unlike Alanine Transaminase (ALT), which is primarily localized in the liver, AST is widely distributed in several tissues, including:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Liver</strong> (highest concentration)</li>
<li><strong>Heart</strong> (cardiac muscle)</li>
<li><strong>Skeletal Muscle</strong></li>
<li><strong>Kidneys</strong></li>
<li><strong>Brain</strong></li>
<li><strong>Red Blood Cells</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>Because of this broad distribution, elevated AST levels are less specific for liver injury than ALT. However, AST is found in both the cytoplasm and mitochondria of cells, whereas ALT is purely cytoplasmic. Therefore, severe tissue necrosis (which damages mitochondria) often leads to a more pronounced rise in AST.</p>

<h2>Testing Information</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Measurement Unit</strong>: Units per Liter (U/L) or International Units per Liter (IU/L)</li>
<li><strong>Sample Type</strong>: Blood (Serum or Plasma)</li>
<li><strong>Fasting Required</strong>: False (though often part of a CMP)</li>
<li><strong>Recommended Test Frequency</strong>: Annually as part of routine screening; more frequent if monitoring liver, heart, or muscle conditions.</li>
</ul>
<h2>💊 Supplements That Affect This Biomarker</h2>
<h3>Reducing AST (Organ Protection)</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/silymarin/milk-thistle/" class="internal-link">Milk Thistle</a> (Silymarin) - Hepatoprotective properties help reduce enzyme leakage from damaged liver cells (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/nac/" class="internal-link">NAC</a> (N-Acetyl Cysteine) - Supports glutathione levels and protects hepatocytes and other cells from oxidative stress (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/curcumin/" class="internal-link">Curcumin</a> - Anti-inflammatory effects can lower AST in patients with liver or muscle inflammation (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 3).</li>
</ul>
<h3>Potentially Elevating AST (Toxicity risk)</h3>
<ul>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Green Tea Extract</a> - High-dose EGCG can be hepatotoxic in some individuals (Variable magnitude, Evidence Level 3).</li>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Anabolic Steroids</a> - Highly associated with liver and muscle stress, leading to significant AST elevations (High magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/ashwagandha/" class="internal-link">Ashwagandha</a> - Rare cases of idiosyncratic liver injury have been reported.</li>
<li><strong>High-Dose Vitamin A</strong> - Chronic retinol toxicity can cause liver damage and elevated AST.</li>
</ul>
