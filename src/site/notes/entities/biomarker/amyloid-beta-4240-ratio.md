<h1>Amyloid-Beta 42/40 Ratio</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The amyloid-beta 42/40 (Abeta42/40) ratio measures the relative concentration of two amyloid-beta peptide isoforms in cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) or blood plasma. Abeta42 is the longer, more aggregation-prone form that preferentially deposits in brain plaques, while Abeta40 is the more abundant but less pathogenic isoform. When amyloid plaques begin accumulating in the brain, Abeta42 is selectively depleted from CSF and plasma, causing the ratio to decrease.</p>
<p>The Abeta42/40 ratio is the cornerstone &quot;A&quot; biomarker in the ATN (Amyloid/Tau/Neurodegeneration) research framework, which defines Alzheimer's disease biologically rather than purely clinically. A positive amyloid biomarker (decreased ratio) indicates brain amyloid pathology regardless of cognitive symptoms, reflecting the growing understanding that Alzheimer's pathology begins 15-20 years before clinical symptoms appear.</p>
<p>CSF Abeta42/40 has historically been the gold standard, measured via lumbar puncture. However, recent advances in mass spectrometry and immunoprecipitation assays have enabled reliable plasma Abeta42/40 measurement, making population-level screening feasible for the first time. Plasma assays (such as PrecivityAD, Simoa, and Lumipulse) now achieve concordance rates of 80-90% with amyloid PET imaging, revolutionizing the diagnostic pathway for Alzheimer's disease.</p>

<p><strong>Notes:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>CSF cutoffs vary slightly by assay platform (Lumipulse, ELISA, mass spectrometry)</li>
<li>Plasma cutoffs are assay-specific and not interchangeable between platforms</li>
<li>The ratio is more diagnostically useful than absolute Abeta42 alone, as it corrects for inter-individual variation in total amyloid production</li>
<li>Age affects cutoffs; some assays provide age-adjusted reference values</li>
<li>A positive result should be confirmed with a second biomarker modality before treatment decisions</li>

<h2>Factors Affecting Levels</h2>
<p><strong>Decrease Abeta42/40 Ratio (pathological):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Brain amyloid plaque deposition (Alzheimer's pathology)</li>
<li>APOE4 genotype (accelerates amyloid accumulation, ratio decreases earlier)</li>
<li>Age (prevalence of amyloid positivity increases with age: ~10% at age 65, ~40% at age 85)</li>
<li>Cerebral amyloid angiopathy (amyloid deposition in blood vessel walls)</li>
<li>Down syndrome (trisomy 21 causes early-onset amyloid pathology)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Increase Abeta42/40 Ratio:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Absence of amyloid pathology (normal aging without Alzheimer's changes)</li>
<li>Effective anti-amyloid therapy (lecanemab, donanemab may partially restore CSF Abeta42)</li>
<li>Younger age</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Pre-analytical factors:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Sample handling is critical -- Abeta peptides adsorb to plastic tubes; use polypropylene</li>
<li>CSF collection timing and storage temperature affect results</li>
<li>Plasma assays require specific blood collection tubes and processing protocols</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<p><strong>Decreased ratio indicates:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Preclinical Alzheimer's disease</strong> -- Amyloid positivity detected by Abeta42/40 precedes cognitive symptoms by 15-20 years. This is the earliest detectable change in the Alzheimer's continuum and defines the &quot;A+&quot; stage in the ATN framework</li>
<li><strong>Mild cognitive impairment due to AD</strong> -- A positive amyloid biomarker in an MCI patient strongly predicts progression to Alzheimer's dementia (positive predictive value ~60-80% over 3-5 years)</li>
<li><strong>Alzheimer's disease dementia</strong> -- &gt;95% of clinically diagnosed AD patients have a decreased Abeta42/40 ratio. A normal ratio in a dementia patient suggests a non-AD etiology</li>
<li><strong>Anti-amyloid treatment eligibility</strong> -- Lecanemab (Leqembi) and donanemab (Kisunla) require confirmed amyloid positivity (PET or CSF/plasma biomarker) before initiation</li>
<li><strong>Differential diagnosis</strong> -- A normal ratio in a patient with cognitive symptoms argues against Alzheimer's and suggests frontotemporal dementia, vascular dementia, or Lewy body dementia</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Clinical pathway for plasma Abeta42/40:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Plasma screening in primary care for at-risk patients (age 65+ with cognitive concerns)</li>
<li>If plasma positive -&gt; confirm with CSF biomarkers or amyloid PET</li>
<li>If confirmed -&gt; evaluate for anti-amyloid therapy eligibility and disease-modifying treatment</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Monitoring use cases:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Alzheimer's disease diagnostic classification (ATN framework)</li>
<li>Anti-amyloid therapy treatment eligibility and monitoring</li>
<li>Clinical trial enrollment and endpoint assessment</li>
<li>Cognitive impairment differential diagnosis</li>
</ul>
<h2>Strategies to Mitigate Risk</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular health</strong> -- Manage hypertension, diabetes, and hyperlipidemia; midlife vascular risk control reduces late-life dementia risk by up to 30%</li>
<li><strong>Regular aerobic exercise</strong> -- 150+ minutes/week is associated with reduced amyloid accumulation and improved cognitive outcomes</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive engagement</strong> -- Lifelong learning, social interaction, and mentally stimulating activities build cognitive reserve that delays clinical expression of amyloid pathology</li>
<li><strong>Sleep optimization</strong> -- Adequate slow-wave sleep promotes glymphatic clearance of brain amyloid; treat sleep apnea which accelerates amyloid accumulation</li>
<li><strong>Mediterranean/MIND diet</strong> -- Plant-rich dietary patterns are associated with lower amyloid burden and reduced Alzheimer's risk</li>
<li><strong>Anti-amyloid therapy</strong> -- For confirmed amyloid-positive patients with early AD, lecanemab and donanemab reduce amyloid plaque burden and slow cognitive decline (requires specialist management, ARIA monitoring)</li>
</ol>
