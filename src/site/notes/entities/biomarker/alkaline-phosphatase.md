<h1>Alkaline Phosphatase</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) is a hydrolase enzyme responsible for removing phosphate groups from many types of molecules, including nucleotides, proteins, and alkaloids. In humans, ALP is present in all tissues throughout the body, but is particularly concentrated in the liver, bile duct, kidney, bone, intestinal mucosa, and the placenta.</p>
<p>The clinical utility of ALP primarily centers on two areas:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Hepatobiliary System:</strong> ALP is present on the canalicular membrane of hepatocytes. In the setting of biliary obstruction (cholestasis), ALP synthesis increases and the enzyme is released into the blood. It is a hallmark marker for obstructive jaundice and infiltrative liver diseases.</li>
<li><strong>Bone Metabolism:</strong> ALP is a byproduct of osteoblast activity (bone formation). Consequently, its levels are physiologically elevated during periods of rapid bone growth (infancy, puberty) and pathologically elevated in conditions of high bone turnover.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Testing Information</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Measurement Unit</strong>: Units per Liter (U/L) or International Units per Liter (IU/L)</li>
<li><strong>Sample Type</strong>: Blood (Serum or Plasma)</li>
<li><strong>Fasting Required</strong>: True (Preferred, as ALP can rise after a fatty meal, particularly in individuals with blood types O and B)</li>
<li><strong>Recommended Test Frequency</strong>: Annually; more frequent if monitoring biliary or bone conditions.</li>
</ul>
<h2>💊 Supplements That Affect This Biomarker</h2>
<h3>Increasing ALP (Addressing Low Levels)</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/zinc/" class="internal-link">Zinc</a> - ALP is a zinc-dependent enzyme; deficiency is a common cause of pathologically low ALP levels (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/magnesium/" class="internal-link">Magnesium</a> - Acts as a cofactor for ALP; deficiency can contribute to low enzyme activity (Moderate magnitude, Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/vitamin-b6/" class="internal-link">Vitamin B6</a> - Deficiency may be associated with low ALP.</li>
</ul>
