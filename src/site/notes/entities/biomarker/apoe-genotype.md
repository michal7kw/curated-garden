<h1>APOE Genotype</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The APOE genotype is a genetic biomarker that identifies the specific isoforms of the Apolipoprotein E protein an individual produces. The APOE gene has three common alleles: ε2, ε3, and ε4. Since everyone inherits one allele from each parent, there are six possible genotypes: ε2/ε2, ε2/ε3, ε2/ε4, ε3/ε3, ε3/ε4, and ε4/ε4.</p>
<p>Apolipoprotein E is a major cholesterol carrier that supports lipid transport and injury repair in the brain and cardiovascular system. The ε3 allele is the most common and is considered &quot;neutral.&quot; The ε2 allele is relatively rare and is generally associated with a lower risk of Alzheimer's disease but an increased risk of Type III hyperlipoproteinemia. The ε4 allele is a well-established risk factor for late-onset Alzheimer's disease and cardiovascular disease, as it is associated with higher LDL cholesterol levels and impaired amyloid-beta clearance in the brain.</p>

<h2>💊 Supplements and Interventions Modulated by APOE Genotype</h2>
<p>While the genotype itself cannot be changed, its clinical implications can be managed through targeted interventions:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/omega-3-fatty-acids/" class="internal-link">Omega-3 Fatty Acids</a> - Research suggests that APOE ε4 carriers may need earlier and higher doses of DHA to achieve neuroprotective benefits, though some studies show reduced efficacy once cognitive decline has started (Evidence Level 4).</li>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/supplement/curcumin/" class="internal-link">Curcumin</a> - May help offset the increased neuroinflammation and oxidative stress associated with the ε4 isoform (Evidence Level 3).</li>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">B-Complex Vitamins</a> - High homocysteine is particularly detrimental in ε4 carriers; maintaining optimal B-vitamin status is critical for synergistic risk reduction (Evidence Level 3).</li>
<li><strong>Saturated Fat Restriction</strong> - ε4 carriers often show greater increases in LDL cholesterol in response to high saturated fat intake compared to ε2 or ε3 carriers.</li>
<li><strong>Physical Exercise</strong> - Regular aerobic exercise has been shown to be particularly effective in reducing Alzheimer's risk among ε4 carriers.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/gene/apoe/" class="internal-link">ApoE</a> Protein - Serum levels of the protein itself can be measured, though genotype provides more specific risk information.</li>
<li><strong>Lipid Panel</strong> - Monitoring LDL and Triglycerides is essential for managing the cardiovascular risks associated with ε4 or ε2 genotypes.</li>
</ul>
