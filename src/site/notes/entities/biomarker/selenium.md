<h1>Selenium</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Trace mineral measured in serum or plasma; cofactor for glutathione peroxidase and thioredoxin reductase (key antioxidant enzymes), iodothyronine deiodinases (thyroid hormone activation), and ~25 selenoproteins. Both deficiency and excess cause clinical syndromes — Keshan disease (deficiency-induced cardiomyopathy) and selenosis (toxic excess) are both well-characterized.</p>
<p>US RDA: 55 mcg/day adults. Major dietary sources: Brazil nuts (~70 mcg per nut), seafood, organ meats, eggs.</p>
