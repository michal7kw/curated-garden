<h1>Acute Kidney Injury Markers</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) markers are a group of protein-based biomarkers used to detect kidney damage earlier and more accurately than traditional measures like serum creatinine. While creatinine is a marker of kidney <em>function</em> (which can take 48-72 hours to reflect injury), these biomarkers indicate actual <em>cellular damage</em> to the kidney tubules, often rising within hours of an insult.</p>
<p>Primary AKI biomarkers include:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>NGAL (Neutrophil Gelatinase-Associated Lipocalin)</strong>: One of the most studied markers, rising rapidly in response to ischemic or toxic injury.</li>
<li><strong>KIM-1 (Kidney Injury Molecule-1)</strong>: Highly specific for proximal tubular damage.</li>
<li><strong>Cystatin C</strong>: A stable marker of GFR that is less affected by muscle mass than creatinine.</li>
<li><strong>TIMP-2 and IGFBP7</strong>: Markers of &quot;cell cycle arrest&quot; that signal early kidney stress before structural damage occurs.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Testing Information</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Measurement Unit</strong>: Varies by marker (ng/mL for NGAL/KIM-1; mg/L for Cystatin C)</li>
<li><strong>Sample Type</strong>: Urine (most AKI-specific markers) or Blood (Cystatin C, NGAL)</li>
<li><strong>Fasting Required</strong>: False</li>
<li><strong>Recommended Test Frequency</strong>: Clinically indicated during acute illness, post-surgery, or when monitoring nephrotoxic medications.</li>
</ul>

<h2>🧪 Lab Tests That Measure This Biomarker</h2>
<h3>Direct Tests</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>NephroCheck</strong> - Specifically measures the TIMP-2 and IGFBP7 ratio for AKI risk.</li>
<li><strong>NGAL ELISA</strong> - Quantitative measurement of NGAL in urine or plasma.</li>
<li><strong>Cystatin C</strong> - Frequently used as a standalone test for more accurate GFR estimation.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Panel Tests</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Kidney Injury Panel</strong> - Comprehensive panels that may include KIM-1, NGAL, and IL-18.</li>
</ul>
