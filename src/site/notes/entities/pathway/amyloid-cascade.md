<h1>Amyloid Cascade</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Amyloid Cascade Hypothesis remains the most dominant (and intensely debated) pathophysiological framework explaining the origin and progression of Alzheimer's Disease. Proposed initially in the early 1990s, the hypothesis posits a strict, linear chronological sequence of catastrophic neurological failures, all explicitly anchored to one single initiating event: the toxic accumulation of the Amyloid-Beta (Aβ) 42 peptide.</p>
<p>Biologically, the cascade functions via sequential dominos:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>APP Cleavage Failure:</strong> The transmembrane precursor protein <a href="/entities/protein/app/" class="internal-link">APP</a> is erroneously cleaved by <em>Beta-Secretase</em> and <em>Gamma-Secretase</em>, shearing off massive quantities of the massively insoluble Aβ42 peptide fragment into the extracellular space.</li>
<li><strong>Oligomerization and Plaque Formation:</strong> Driven by complex kinetics and failed clearance (e.g., poor sleep disrupting the glymphatic system), these free-floating Aβ peptides instantly misfold into toxic oligomers, eventually precipitating out of solution into massive, crystallized extracellular <strong>Amyloid Plaques</strong>.</li>
<li><strong>Downstream Tau Activation:</strong> The physical toxicity of these plaques (or specifically the free-floating oligomeric precursors) violently stresses the surrounding neurons. This stress natively triggers massive intracellular kinase activation, forcing the downstream <a href="/entities/pathway/tau-phosphorylation/" class="internal-link">Tau Phosphorylation</a> pathway to violently rip Tau scaffolding off the cell's microtubules, forming internal Neurofibrillary Tangles.</li>
<li><strong>Microglial Activation:</strong> The massive protein tangles inherently trigger a localized immune system explosion (<a href="/entities/pathway/neuroinflammation/" class="internal-link">Neuroinflammation</a>), activating microglia that violently release cytokines, ultimately driving massive neuronal apoptosis and gray matter atrophy.</li>
</ol>
<h2>Clinical Pathologies</h2>
<p>This pathway is arguably the most heavily targeted pharmaceutical target in modern medical history. Almost all recent landmark monoclonal antibodies for Alzheimer's (e.g., <em>Lecanemab</em>, <em>Aducanumab</em>, <em>Donanemab</em>) explicitly target and clear these Amyloid-Beta monomers/plaques to artificially halt the cascade.</p>
