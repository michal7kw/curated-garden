<h1>Tuberoinfundibular Dopamine Pathway</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Central Nervous System contains exactly four cardinal dopamine pathways heavily regulating human biology. While the mesolimbic pathway entirely dictates reward/addiction and the nigrostriatal pathway physically commands voluntary movement, the <strong>Tuberoinfundibular pathway</strong> operates entirely divorced from both thought and movement. Instead, it is an incredibly short, rigid, isolated track serving as an explicit endocrine command cable.</p>
<p>Running aggressively from the accurate nucleus resting at the base of the hypothalamus directly down into the median eminence of the infundibulum (the pituitary stalk), this dopamine pathway constantly secretes dopamine directly into the hypophyseal portal blood supply feeding the anterior pituitary gland.</p>
<p>When this dopamine hits the D2 receptors lining the massive lactotroph cells sitting inside the pituitary, it fundamentally <strong>inhibits</strong> the release of <a href="/entities/labtest/prolactin/" class="internal-link">Prolactin</a>. The resting human brain is constantly firing dopamine down this pathway to keep Prolactin safely offline (preventing unauthorized galactorrhea). Only specific physiological triggers like intense infant suckling neurologically shut this dopamine pathway off, releasing the brakes and permitting massive Prolactin floods for lactation.</p>
<h2>Clinical Pathologies</h2>
<p>This pathway holds massive relevance strictly in regards to <strong>Antipsychotic Pharmacology</strong>. Because traditional Typical Antipsychotic drugs (like Haloperidol) mathematically require completely locking up D2 dopamine receptors to resolve schizophrenia across the brain, they inadvertently blockade the D2 receptors natively attached to this tubing pathway.</p>
<p>With the dopamine securely blocked from binding, the pituitary lactotrophs are suddenly released from inhibition, violently skyrocketing Prolactin plasma concentrations (a clinical disaster specifically called massive drug-induced hyperprolactinemia). Due to intricate endocrine feedback loops, this insane Prolactin spike brutally suppresses the entire HPG axis, triggering immediate amenorrhea in females, devastating gynecomastia/galactorrhea, and massively depressed plasma logic causing total systemic sexual dysfunction in both sexes.</p>
