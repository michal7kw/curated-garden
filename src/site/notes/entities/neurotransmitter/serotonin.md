<h1>Serotonin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Monoamine neurotransmitter regulating mood, sleep, appetite, and cognition. Approximately 90% is produced in the gut. Synthesized from tryptophan.</p>
<p><strong>Chemical Formula:</strong> C10H12N2O<br />
<strong>Molecular Weight:</strong> 176.21</p>
<h2>Primary Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Mood regulation</li>
<li>Sleep-wake cycle</li>
<li>Appetite control</li>
<li>Pain modulation</li>
<li>Cognitive function</li>
</ul>
<h2>Receptor Types</h2>
<ul>
<li>5-HT1A (anxiolytic, autoreceptor)</li>
<li>5-HT1B (vasoconstriction, autoreceptor)</li>
<li>5-HT2A (psychedelic effects, platelet aggregation)</li>
<li>5-HT2C (appetite, anxiety)</li>
<li>5-HT3 (nausea, ion channel)</li>
<li>5-HT4 (GI motility, cognition)</li>
<li>5-HT6 (cognition, memory)</li>
<li>5-HT7 (circadian rhythm, thermoregulation)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Synthesis Pathway</h2>
<p>Tryptophan → 5-HTP (tryptophan hydroxylase) → Serotonin (aromatic amino acid decarboxylase)</p>
<h2>Degradation Pathway</h2>
<p>Serotonin → 5-HIAA (MAO-A)</p>
<h2>Associated Conditions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Depression (serotonin deficiency hypothesis)</li>
<li>Anxiety disorders (5-HT1A dysregulation)</li>
<li>OCD (serotonergic circuit dysfunction)</li>
<li>Migraine (5-HT vasoconstriction)</li>
<li>IBS (gut serotonin dysregulation)</li>
</ul>
