<h1>Glycine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Simplest amino acid that serves as a major inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brainstem and spinal cord. Also acts as a co-agonist at NMDA glutamate receptors (excitatory role). Dual inhibitory/excitatory function depending on receptor type. Important for collagen synthesis, glutathione production, and sleep quality.</p>
<h2>Dual Role</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Inhibitory</strong>: Glycine receptor (GlyR) in brainstem and spinal cord — motor control, sensory processing</li>
<li><strong>Excitatory</strong>: NMDA receptor co-agonist in cortex and hippocampus — synaptic plasticity, learning</li>
</ul>
<h2>Sleep Effects</h2>
<ul>
<li>3g before bed reduces core body temperature and improves subjective sleep quality</li>
<li>Acts on NMDA receptors in suprachiasmatic nucleus to promote vasodilation and heat loss</li>
</ul>
