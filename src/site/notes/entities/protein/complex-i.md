<h1>Complex I</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Complex I (NADH:ubiquinone oxidoreductase) is the largest and first enzyme complex of the mitochondrial <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/electron-transport-chain/" class="internal-link">Electron Transport Chain</a>. With 45 subunits (~980 kDa), it is an L-shaped structure with a hydrophilic arm extending into the mitochondrial matrix and a hydrophobic arm embedded in the inner membrane.</p>
<p>Complex I is the entry point for electrons from NADH generated by the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/citric-acid-cycle/" class="internal-link">Citric Acid Cycle</a>, fatty acid beta-oxidation, and other matrix dehydrogenases. It transfers these electrons to ubiquinone (<a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">CoQ10</a>) while coupling this to proton translocation.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<h3>Electron Transfer</h3>
<ol>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">NADH</a> binds and donates 2 electrons to FMN (flavin mononucleotide)</li>
<li>Electrons pass through 7 iron-sulfur (Fe-S) clusters</li>
<li>Terminal Fe-S cluster N2 reduces ubiquinone to ubiquinol (QH2)</li>
<li>QH2 diffuses within the membrane to <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/complex-iii/" class="internal-link">Complex III</a></li>
</ol>
<h3>Proton Pumping</h3>
<ul>
<li>4 H+ pumped per NADH oxidized (matrix to intermembrane space)</li>
<li>Driven by long-range conformational coupling through the membrane arm</li>
<li>Three antiporter-like subunits (ND2, ND4, ND5) each translocate 1 H+</li>
<li>Fourth proton pumped at the ubiquinone-binding site</li>
</ul>
<h3>ROS Generation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Major site of <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Reactive Oxygen Species</a> production</li>
<li>Superoxide generated when electrons leak to O2 at FMN or Fe-S clusters</li>
<li>Increased during reverse electron transport (high QH2/Q ratio)</li>
<li>Contributes to mitochondrial oxidative stress</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Leigh syndrome</strong>: Mutations in Complex I subunits (most common cause)</li>
<li><strong>Leber hereditary optic neuropathy (LHON)</strong>: mtDNA mutations in ND1, ND4, ND6</li>
<li><strong>Parkinson's disease</strong>: Complex I inhibition by environmental toxins (rotenone, MPTP)</li>
<li><strong>Aging</strong>: Progressive decline in Complex I activity with age</li>
<li><strong>Metformin</strong>: Mild Complex I inhibitor, contributes to its metabolic effects</li>
<li><strong>Ischemia-reperfusion</strong>: Reverse electron transport at Complex I drives ROS burst</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Connections</h2>
<h3>Substrates</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">NADH</a></strong>: Electron donor (oxidized to NAD+)</li>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">CoQ10</a></strong> (ubiquinone): Electron acceptor (reduced to ubiquinol)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Products</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Reactive Oxygen Species</a></strong>: Superoxide from electron leak (~0.1-2%)</li>
<li><strong>Proton gradient</strong>: Contributes to proton motive force for <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/atp-synthase/" class="internal-link">ATP Synthase</a></li>
</ul>
