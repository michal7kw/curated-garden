<h1>Phosphofructokinase</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1) catalyzes step 3 of <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/glycolysis/" class="internal-link">Glycolysis</a>, the phosphorylation of fructose-6-phosphate to fructose-1,6-bisphosphate. This is the committed step of glycolysis (the true &quot;point of no return&quot;) and the primary site of glycolytic regulation.</p>
<p>PFK-1 functions as a homotetramer with three tissue-specific isoforms:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>PFK-M</strong> (muscle): Dominant in skeletal muscle</li>
<li><strong>PFK-L</strong> (liver): Dominant in liver</li>
<li><strong>PFK-P</strong> (platelet): Widely expressed, dominant in brain</li>
</ul>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<h3>Catalytic Reaction</h3>
<ul>
<li>Fructose-6-phosphate + <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">ATP</a> -&gt; Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate + ADP</li>
<li>Irreversible under physiological conditions</li>
<li>Requires Mg2+ as cofactor</li>
</ul>

<h3>Fructose-2,6-bisphosphate (F2,6BP)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Most potent allosteric activator of PFK-1</li>
<li>Produced by PFK-2 (bifunctional enzyme: kinase/phosphatase)</li>
<li>Insulin activates PFK-2 kinase activity (increases F2,6BP)</li>
<li>Glucagon activates PFK-2 phosphatase activity (decreases F2,6BP)</li>
<li>This is how hormones regulate glycolytic flux</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Tarui disease (GSD VII)</strong>: PFK-M deficiency causes exercise intolerance, hemolytic anemia</li>
<li><strong>Warburg effect</strong>: Cancer cells show increased PFK-1 activity; TIGAR (p53-regulated) modulates F2,6BP</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic syndrome</strong>: Altered PFK regulation in insulin resistance</li>
<li><strong>Exercise physiology</strong>: PFK-1 is the major bottleneck limiting glycolytic flux in muscle</li>
<li><strong>pH sensitivity</strong>: Protective mechanism against lactic acidosis during intense exercise</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Connections</h2>
<h3>Substrates</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Fructose-6-phosphate</strong>: From <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Glucose-6-Phosphate</a> isomerization</li>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">ATP</a></strong>: Phosphate donor (also allosteric inhibitor at high levels)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Products</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate</strong>: Committed glycolytic intermediate</li>
<li><strong>ADP</strong>: Recycled by oxidative phosphorylation</li>
</ul>
