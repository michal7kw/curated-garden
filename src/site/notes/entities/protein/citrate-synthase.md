<h1>Citrate Synthase</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Citrate synthase (CS) is the first enzyme of the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/citric-acid-cycle/" class="internal-link">Citric Acid Cycle</a>, catalyzing the condensation of <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Acetyl-CoA</a> (2 carbons) with <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Oxaloacetate</a> (4 carbons) to produce <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Citrate</a> (6 carbons). This reaction initiates each turn of the TCA cycle and is the primary entry point for acetyl groups derived from carbohydrates, fats, and amino acids.</p>
<p>Citrate synthase is a homodimer (~100 kDa) and is one of the most conserved enzymes in biology. Its activity is widely used as a biomarker for mitochondrial content in tissue samples and as a normalizer for other mitochondrial enzyme activities.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<h3>Catalytic Steps</h3>
<ol>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Oxaloacetate</a> binds first (ordered binding), inducing conformational change</li>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Acetyl-CoA</a> binds to the closed form</li>
<li>Claisen condensation: methyl carbon of acetyl-CoA attacks carbonyl of oxaloacetate</li>
<li>Citryl-CoA intermediate formed</li>
<li>Hydrolysis releases <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Citrate</a> and free <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Coenzyme A</a></li>
</ol>
<h3>Energetics</h3>
<ul>
<li>delta-G = -31.5 kJ/mol (highly favorable)</li>
<li>Energy comes from thioester bond hydrolysis of citryl-CoA</li>
<li>Effectively irreversible, pulls the cycle forward</li>
</ul>
<h3>Regulation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Substrate availability</strong>: Oxaloacetate and acetyl-CoA concentrations are primary regulators</li>
<li><strong>Product inhibition</strong>: Citrate inhibits citrate synthase</li>
<li><strong>ATP</strong>: Allosteric inhibitor (signals sufficient energy)</li>
<li><strong>NADH</strong>: Indirectly inhibits by limiting oxaloacetate (via malate dehydrogenase equilibrium)</li>
<li><strong>Succinyl-CoA</strong>: Competitive inhibitor (competes with acetyl-CoA)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mitochondrial biomarker</strong>: CS activity is the gold standard for mitochondrial content in muscle biopsies</li>
<li><strong>Exercise training</strong>: Endurance training increases CS activity 50-100% (marker of mitochondrial biogenesis)</li>
<li><strong>Aging</strong>: CS activity declines with age, reflecting mitochondrial loss</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic disease</strong>: Reduced CS in obesity, type 2 diabetes, and mitochondrial myopathies</li>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong>: Some tumors show altered CS expression; citrate export supports lipogenesis</li>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/pgc-1alpha/" class="internal-link">PGC-1alpha</a></strong>: Master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis, upregulates CS expression</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Connections</h2>
<h3>Substrates</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Acetyl-CoA</a></strong>: From <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/pyruvate-dehydrogenase/" class="internal-link">Pyruvate Dehydrogenase</a>, fatty acid oxidation, or amino acid catabolism</li>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Oxaloacetate</a></strong>: Regenerated by malate dehydrogenase (step 8 of TCA)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Products</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Citrate</a></strong>: First TCA intermediate, also exported for lipogenesis</li>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Coenzyme A</a></strong>: Recycled for acetyl-CoA production</li>
</ul>
