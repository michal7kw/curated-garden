<h1>Complex II</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Complex II (succinate dehydrogenase, SDH) is unique among the electron transport chain complexes: it is the only enzyme that participates directly in both the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/citric-acid-cycle/" class="internal-link">Citric Acid Cycle</a> and the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/electron-transport-chain/" class="internal-link">Electron Transport Chain</a>. It is also the smallest ETC complex (~124 kDa) and the only one entirely encoded by nuclear DNA (no mtDNA subunits).</p>
<p>Complex II consists of four subunits: SDHA and SDHB form the catalytic domain in the matrix, while SDHC and SDHD anchor the complex in the inner mitochondrial membrane and provide the ubiquinone-binding site.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<h3>Dual Function</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>TCA cycle</strong>: Oxidizes <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Succinate</a> to <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Fumarate</a> (step 6 of TCA)</li>
<li><strong>ETC</strong>: Transfers electrons from <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">FADH2</a> to ubiquinone (<a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">CoQ10</a>)</li>
</ol>
<h3>Electron Transfer Pathway</h3>
<ol>
<li>Succinate binds to SDHA and is oxidized to fumarate</li>
<li>FAD (covalently bound to SDHA) is reduced to FADH2</li>
<li>Electrons transfer through 3 Fe-S clusters in SDHB</li>
<li>Ubiquinone at the SDHC/SDHD interface is reduced to ubiquinol</li>
</ol>
<h3>No Proton Pumping</h3>
<ul>
<li>Complex II does NOT translocate protons across the membrane</li>
<li>This is why FADH2-linked substrates yield ~1.5 ATP vs ~2.5 ATP for NADH</li>
<li>Ubiquinol produced feeds into <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/complex-iii/" class="internal-link">Complex III</a> for further electron transfer</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Paraganglioma/Pheochromocytoma</strong>: Germline mutations in SDHB, SDHC, SDHD (tumor suppressor)</li>
<li><strong>Gastrointestinal stromal tumors (GIST)</strong>: SDH-deficient subtype</li>
<li><strong>Leigh syndrome</strong>: Rare SDHA mutations cause neurodegeneration</li>
<li><strong>Carney-Stratakis syndrome</strong>: SDH mutations with paraganglioma + GIST</li>
<li><strong>Cancer metabolism</strong>: Succinate accumulation (oncometabolite) inhibits prolyl hydroxylases, stabilizing HIF-1alpha</li>
<li><strong>Aging</strong>: Complex II is relatively preserved with aging compared to other complexes</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Connections</h2>
<h3>Substrates</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Succinate</a></strong>: TCA cycle intermediate, electron donor</li>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">CoQ10</a></strong> (ubiquinone): Electron acceptor</li>
</ul>
<h3>Products</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Fumarate</a></strong>: TCA cycle product</li>
<li><strong>Ubiquinol (QH2)</strong>: Carries electrons to <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/complex-iii/" class="internal-link">Complex III</a></li>
</ul>
