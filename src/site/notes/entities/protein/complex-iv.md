<h1>Complex IV</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Complex IV (cytochrome c oxidase, COX) is the terminal oxidase of the mitochondrial <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/electron-transport-chain/" class="internal-link">Electron Transport Chain</a>, catalyzing the final transfer of electrons to molecular oxygen. It is the largest complex in the inner mitochondrial membrane, containing 13 subunits in mammals, with 3 core catalytic subunits (COX I, II, III) encoded by mitochondrial DNA.</p>
<p>This enzyme is where ~95% of all oxygen consumed by the body is utilized, making it the direct link between the ETC and aerobic respiration.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<h3>Electron Transfer</h3>
<ol>
<li><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/cytochrome-c/" class="internal-link">Cytochrome C</a> (reduced) docks and donates one electron to CuA (in COX II)</li>
<li>Electron transfers to heme a (in COX I)</li>
<li>Electron moves to binuclear center: heme a3 + CuB (in COX I)</li>
<li>Four sequential electron transfers fully reduce O2 to 2 H2O</li>
</ol>
<h3>Oxygen Reduction</h3>
<ul>
<li>O2 binds at the heme a3-CuB binuclear center</li>
<li>Four electrons reduce O2 stepwise (avoiding release of toxic intermediates)</li>
<li>4 H+ consumed from matrix for water formation (&quot;chemical&quot; protons)</li>
<li>4 H+ pumped to intermembrane space (&quot;pumped&quot; protons)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Proton Translocation</h3>
<ul>
<li>2 H+ pumped per electron pair (4 total per O2)</li>
<li>Combined with 4 &quot;chemical&quot; protons = 8 H+ removed from matrix per O2</li>
<li>Contributes significantly to the proton motive force</li>
</ul>
<h2>Regulation</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Allosteric ATP inhibition</strong>: High ATP/ADP ratio slows Complex IV</li>
<li><strong>Nitric oxide (NO)</strong>: Competitive, reversible inhibitor at O2 binding site</li>
<li><strong>Thyroid hormone</strong>: Upregulates Complex IV expression</li>
<li><strong>Calcium</strong>: Activates Complex IV via dephosphorylation</li>
<li><strong>Red light therapy</strong>: 660-850nm light may relieve NO inhibition of Complex IV</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cyanide poisoning</strong>: CN- binds irreversibly to heme a3, blocking O2 reduction</li>
<li><strong>Carbon monoxide poisoning</strong>: CO competes with O2 at binuclear center</li>
<li><strong>Leigh syndrome</strong>: COX assembly defects (SURF1 mutations)</li>
<li><strong>MELAS/MERRF</strong>: mtDNA mutations affecting COX subunits</li>
<li><strong>Aging</strong>: Complex IV activity declines, contributing to mitochondrial dysfunction</li>
<li><strong>Neurodegenerative disease</strong>: Reduced Complex IV activity in Alzheimer's</li>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/intervention/red-light-therapy/" class="internal-link">Red Light Therapy</a></strong>: Proposed mechanism via photodissociation of NO from CuB</li>
</ul>

</ul>

<ul>

</ul>
