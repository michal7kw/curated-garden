<h1>Leaky Gut</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Increased intestinal permeability allowing bacterial endotoxins (LPS), undigested food particles, and antigens to cross the gut barrier into systemic circulation. Triggers immune activation and chronic low-grade inflammation. Formally termed &quot;increased intestinal permeability&quot; — recognized as a contributing factor in autoimmune diseases, metabolic syndrome, and neuroinflammation.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<ul>
<li>Tight junction proteins (claudins, occludin, ZO-1) become disrupted</li>
<li>Zonulin release (triggered by gluten, bacteria) opens paracellular pathway</li>
<li>LPS translocation activates TLR4 → NF-kB → systemic inflammation</li>
<li>Immune system encounters novel antigens → food sensitivities, molecular mimicry</li>
</ul>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Bloating and gas</li>
<li>New food sensitivities</li>
<li>Fatigue and brain fog</li>
<li>Joint pain</li>
<li>Skin issues (eczema, acne)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment</h2>
<ul>
<li>Elimination diet (remove triggers)</li>
<li>L-Glutamine (gut barrier repair)</li>
<li>Probiotics/prebiotics</li>
<li>Zinc carnosine</li>
<li>Butyrate supplementation</li>
<li>Stress reduction</li>
</ul>
