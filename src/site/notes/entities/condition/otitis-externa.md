<h1>Otitis Externa</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Otitis Externa (Swimmer's Ear) is an inflammation, irritation, or infection of the outer ear and ear canal. It usually occurs when water stays in the ear canal for a long time, creating a moist environment that aids bacterial growth. It can also be caused by trauma to the lining of the ear canal (e.g., from cotton swabs).</p>
<h2>Causes</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bacteria:</strong> <em>Pseudomonas aeruginosa</em> (common in water), <em>Staphylococcus aureus</em>.</li>
<li><strong>Fungi:</strong> <em>Candida</em>, <em>Aspergillus</em> (less common).</li>
</ul>
