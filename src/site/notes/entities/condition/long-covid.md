<h1>Long COVID</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Multi-system condition occurring after SARS-CoV-2 infection, defined by WHO as symptoms persisting or developing 3+ months after initial infection, lasting at least 2 months, and not explained by alternative diagnoses. Affects an estimated 10-36% of COVID-19 survivors. Involves viral persistence, autoimmunity, microclotting, and autonomic dysfunction.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Viral persistence</strong>: SARS-CoV-2 RNA/protein persists in gut, brain, and other tissues</li>
<li><strong>Autoimmunity</strong>: Molecular mimicry triggers autoantibodies (anti-GPCR, antinuclear)</li>
<li><strong>Microclotting</strong>: Spike protein induces amyloid fibrin microclots trapping inflammatory molecules</li>
<li><strong>Immune dysregulation</strong>: Chronic T-cell activation, monocyte activation, mast cell dysfunction</li>
<li><strong>Autonomic dysfunction</strong>: Vagus nerve inflammation → dysautonomia / POTS</li>
</ul>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic fatigue and post-exertional malaise</li>
<li>Cognitive dysfunction (brain fog)</li>
<li>Dyspnea and exercise intolerance</li>
<li>Dysautonomia (POTS, orthostatic intolerance)</li>
<li>Pain (headaches, joint, muscle)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment</h2>
<ul>
<li>Pacing and energy management</li>
<li>Antihistamines (H1 + H2 blockers)</li>
<li>Low-dose naltrexone</li>
<li>Anti-inflammatory supplements (omega-3, NAC, quercetin)</li>
<li>Autonomic rehabilitation</li>
</ul>
