<h1>Burnout Syndrome</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>State of chronic physical and emotional exhaustion resulting from prolonged occupational or caregiving stress. Classified by WHO (ICD-11) as an occupational phenomenon characterized by emotional exhaustion, depersonalization/cynicism, and reduced personal accomplishment. Associated with HPA axis dysregulation, chronic inflammation, and autonomic dysfunction.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Early stage</strong>: HPA axis hyperactivation → elevated cortisol → anxiety, insomnia</li>
<li><strong>Late stage</strong>: HPA axis exhaustion → flattened cortisol curve, depleted DHEA → fatigue, apathy</li>
<li>Chronic sympathetic overdrive → reduced HRV, autonomic dysfunction</li>
<li>Neuroinflammation → reduced BDNF → cognitive impairment</li>
</ul>
<h2>Three Dimensions (Maslach)</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Emotional exhaustion</strong>: Feeling drained, unable to cope</li>
<li><strong>Depersonalization/Cynicism</strong>: Detachment, negative attitudes</li>
<li><strong>Reduced efficacy</strong>: Decreased productivity, loss of competence feeling</li>
</ol>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic fatigue unrelieved by rest</li>
<li>Emotional exhaustion and cynicism</li>
<li>Cognitive impairment and brain fog</li>
<li>Insomnia and sleep disturbance</li>
<li>Physical symptoms (headaches, GI, muscle tension)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment</h2>
<ul>
<li>Workplace modifications</li>
<li>CBT and professional support</li>
<li>Regular aerobic exercise</li>
<li>Sleep optimization</li>
<li>Mindfulness-based stress reduction</li>
<li>Adaptogenic supplements (ashwagandha, rhodiola)</li>
</ul>
