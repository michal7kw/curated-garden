<h1>Acute Pain</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Acute pain is a normal physiological response to an adverse chemical, thermal, or mechanical stimulus. It is self-limiting and resolves when the underlying cause is treated or healed. Unlike chronic pain, it serves a protective biological function by warning of injury.</p>
<h2>Pathophysiology</h2>
<p>It involves <strong>nociception</strong>, where specialized nerve endings (nociceptors) detect noxious stimuli and transmit signals via A-delta and C fibers to the spinal cord and brain.</p>
