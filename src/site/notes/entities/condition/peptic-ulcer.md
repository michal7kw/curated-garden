<h1>Peptic Ulcer</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Peptic ulcers are open sores that develop on the inside lining of the stomach (<strong>Gastric Ulcer</strong>) and the upper portion of the small intestine (<strong>Duodenal Ulcer</strong>). The most common causes are infection with the bacterium <em>Helicobacter pylori</em> (<em>H. pylori</em>) and long-term use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen and naproxen sodium. Stress and spicy foods do not cause peptic ulcers but can make symptoms worse.</p>
