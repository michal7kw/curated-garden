<h1>Hashimotos Thyroiditis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Hashimoto's Thyroiditis (Chronic Lymphocytic Thyroiditis) is the leading cause of <strong>Hypothyroidism</strong> in developed countries. It is characterized by thyroid inflammation and the presence of antibodies against thyroid peroxidase (<strong>TPO</strong>) or thyroglobulin.</p>
<h2>Pathophysiology</h2>
<p>Infiltration of the thyroid gland by lymphocytes leading to follicular destruction and fibrosis. This gradually reduces the gland's ability to produce thyroid hormones.</p>
