<h1>Ankylosing Spondylitis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A type of arthritis that causes inflammation in the joints and ligaments of the spine, potentially leading to spinal fusion.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic low back pain and stiffness (worse in morning, improves with exercise)</li>
<li>Sacroiliitis, progressive spinal fusion, enthesitis, uveitis (25%)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Exercise (cornerstone)</strong>: Daily stretching -- prevents spinal fusion</li>
<li><strong>NSAIDs (first-line)</strong>: Continuous use may slow radiographic progression</li>
<li><strong>Biologics</strong>: TNF inhibitors (adalimumab), IL-17 inhibitors (secukinumab)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3, vitamin D, curcumin, probiotics</li>
</ul>
