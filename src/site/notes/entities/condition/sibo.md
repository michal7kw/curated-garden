<h1>SIBO</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth — excessive bacterial colonization of the small intestine. Three subtypes: hydrogen-dominant (diarrhea), methane-dominant/IMO (constipation), and hydrogen sulfide (mixed). Causes bloating, malabsorption, and systemic inflammation. Often comorbid with IBS and motility disorders. High recurrence rate without addressing root cause.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Motility failure</strong>: Impaired migrating motor complex (MMC) allows bacterial accumulation</li>
<li><strong>Fermentation</strong>: Bacteria ferment carbohydrates → hydrogen, methane, or H2S gas</li>
<li><strong>Methane effects</strong>: Methane directly slows transit → constipation cycle</li>
<li><strong>Bile acid deconjugation</strong>: Bacteria deconjugate bile salts → fat malabsorption</li>
<li><strong>Mucosal damage</strong>: Bacterial metabolites damage intestinal epithelium → permeability</li>
</ul>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Bloating and abdominal distension (hallmark)</li>
<li>Diarrhea or constipation (subtype-dependent)</li>
<li>Abdominal pain and cramping</li>
<li>Fatigue and brain fog</li>
<li>Nutrient deficiency signs</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Hydrogen-dominant</strong>: Rifaximin 550mg TID x 14 days</li>
<li><strong>Methane-dominant</strong>: Rifaximin + neomycin/metronidazole</li>
<li><strong>Herbal alternative</strong>: Berberine, oregano oil, neem</li>
<li><strong>Post-treatment</strong>: Prokinetics + meal spacing to prevent recurrence</li>
</ul>
