<h1>Depression</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Major depressive disorder (MDD) is far more than &quot;feeling sad&quot; — it is a systemic illness involving neurotransmitter dysregulation, HPA axis hyperactivity, neuroinflammation, impaired neuroplasticity, and altered gut-brain axis signaling. The monoamine hypothesis (serotonin/norepinephrine deficiency) dominated for decades but is now recognized as incomplete. Modern understanding emphasizes glutamate/GABA imbalance, BDNF-mediated neuroplasticity deficits (explaining ketamine's rapid antidepressant effect), and the inflammatory hypothesis (elevated IL-6, TNF-alpha, CRP in ~30% of patients). Depression doubles cardiovascular disease risk and is the leading cause of disability worldwide (WHO).</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Core symptoms</strong> (required for diagnosis): Depressed mood OR anhedonia (loss of interest/pleasure) lasting 2+ weeks</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive</strong>: Poor concentration, indecisiveness, excessive guilt, worthlessness, suicidal ideation</li>
<li><strong>Physical</strong>: Sleep disturbance (insomnia or hypersomnia), appetite/weight change, psychomotor retardation or agitation, fatigue</li>
<li><strong>Social</strong>: Withdrawal from relationships and activities, decreased productivity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Subtypes</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)</strong> — Classic presentation, episodic</li>
<li><strong>Persistent Depressive Disorder (PDD/Dysthymia)</strong> — Chronic low-grade depression for 2+ years</li>
<li><strong>Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)</strong> — Winter-onset, light-responsive</li>
<li><strong>Postpartum Depression</strong> — Onset within 4 weeks of delivery</li>
<li><strong>Atypical Depression</strong> — Mood reactivity, hypersomnia, hyperphagia, leaden paralysis</li>
<li><strong>Treatment-Resistant Depression</strong> — Failure of 2+ adequate antidepressant trials</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Family history (40-50% heritable)</li>
<li>Childhood adversity and trauma</li>
<li>Chronic stress and HPA axis dysregulation</li>
<li>Social isolation and loneliness</li>
<li>Chronic inflammation (elevated CRP &gt;3 mg/L)</li>
<li>Vitamin D deficiency, omega-3 deficiency</li>
<li>Sedentary lifestyle</li>
<li>Substance abuse</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>First-line pharmacotherapy</strong>: SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline), SNRIs (venlafaxine, duloxetine)</li>
<li><strong>Psychotherapy</strong>: CBT, behavioral activation, interpersonal therapy</li>
<li><strong>Exercise</strong>: 150 min/week moderate exercise — effect size comparable to antidepressants</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Sleep optimization, Mediterranean diet, social connection, light exposure</li>
<li><strong>Treatment-resistant</strong>: Ketamine/esketamine, TMS, ECT, MAOIs, augmentation strategies</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 (EPA-dominant), vitamin D, SAMe, 5-HTP (adjunctive)</li>
</ul>
