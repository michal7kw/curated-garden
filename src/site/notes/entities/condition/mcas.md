<h1>MCAS</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Mast Cell Activation Syndrome — chronic condition characterized by inappropriate and excessive mast cell activation and mediator release affecting multiple organ systems. Distinct from mastocytosis (clonal proliferation). Episodic multisystem symptoms triggered by various stimuli. Diagnosis requires the Vienna consensus triad: symptoms, elevated mediators, and treatment response.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mast cell degranulation</strong>: Inappropriate activation releases histamine, prostaglandins, leukotrienes, heparin, cytokines</li>
<li><strong>Multi-organ involvement</strong>: Mast cells reside in skin, gut, lungs, bone marrow — activation affects all tissues</li>
<li><strong>Neurogenic inflammation</strong>: Mast cell-nerve interactions amplify symptoms</li>
<li><strong>Immune dysregulation</strong>: Cross-talk with T-cells, eosinophils, and innate immune system</li>
</ul>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Flushing, urticaria, and skin rashes</li>
<li>GI distress (pain, diarrhea, nausea)</li>
<li>Tachycardia and blood pressure instability</li>
<li>Brain fog and cognitive dysfunction</li>
<li>Fatigue and malaise</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment</h2>
<ul>
<li>H1 + H2 antihistamines (foundation of treatment)</li>
<li>Mast cell stabilizers (cromolyn, ketotifen)</li>
<li>Leukotriene inhibitors (montelukast)</li>
<li>Low-histamine diet + DAO supplementation</li>
<li>Quercetin and vitamin C (natural mast cell support)</li>
</ul>
