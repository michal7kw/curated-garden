<h1>Systemic Lupus Erythematosus</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A chronic autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks various tissues, causing widespread inflammation and tissue damage in multiple organs.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Fatigue and fever</li>
<li>Joint pain and stiffness (non-erosive polyarthritis)</li>
<li>Butterfly-shaped (malar) rash on the face</li>
<li>Photosensitivity (sun exposure triggers flares)</li>
<li>Raynaud's phenomenon (fingers turning white/blue in cold)</li>
<li>Chest pain (pleurisy or pericarditis)</li>
<li>Oral ulcers</li>
<li>Hair loss (alopecia)</li>
<li>Kidney involvement (lupus nephritis — proteinuria, hematuria)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Antimalarials</strong>: Hydroxychloroquine — cornerstone of SLE therapy. Reduces flares by 50%, improves survival, prevents organ damage. Should be continued in all SLE patients unless contraindicated</li>
<li><strong>Corticosteroids</strong>: Prednisone for acute flares — lowest effective dose, shortest duration. Pulse IV methylprednisolone for severe flares (nephritis, cerebritis)</li>
<li><strong>Immunosuppressives</strong>: Mycophenolate mofetil or cyclophosphamide (for lupus nephritis), azathioprine (maintenance), tacrolimus. Belimumab (anti-BLyS — FDA-approved biologic), voclosporin (for nephritis)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Vitamin D (target &gt;40 ng/mL — most SLE patients are deficient, immunomodulatory), omega-3 fatty acids (reduce inflammation and cardiovascular risk), NAC (glutathione support), curcumin (anti-inflammatory), selenium (antioxidant)</li>
<li><strong>Sun protection</strong>: Strict photoprotection — broad-spectrum SPF 50+, protective clothing, avoid peak UV hours. UV exposure is a major flare trigger</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Anti-inflammatory diet, regular gentle-to-moderate exercise (improves fatigue and cardiovascular fitness), stress management, adequate sleep, smoking cessation (worsens disease activity and accelerates atherosclerosis)</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: Regular renal function (creatinine, urinalysis, protein-to-creatinine ratio), complement levels (C3, C4), anti-dsDNA titers, CBC</li>
</ul>
