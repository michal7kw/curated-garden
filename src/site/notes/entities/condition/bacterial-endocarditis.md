<h1>Bacterial Endocarditis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Bacterial Endocarditis (Infective Endocarditis) occurs when bacteria enter the bloodstream and attach to damaged areas of the heart, forming vegetations (clumps of bacteria and cells). If untreated, it can destroy heart valves and cause life-threatening complications like heart failure and stroke (from emboli).</p>
<h2>Etiology</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Staphylococcus aureus:</strong> Common in acute cases and IV drug users.</li>
<li><strong>Streptococcus viridans:</strong> Common in subacute cases, often from oral flora.</li>
<li><strong>Enterococci:</strong> Associated with GU/GI tract procedures.</li>
</ul>
