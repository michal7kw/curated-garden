<h1>Cognitive Dysfunction</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Cognitive Dysfunction (or Cognitive Impairment) is a broad term describing a decline in mental abilities. It ranges from mild subjective complaints (&quot;brain fog&quot;) to severe dementia. It is not a specific disease but a symptom or manifestation of various underlying pathologies, including neurodegeneration, vascular damage, metabolic imbalances, or psychiatric conditions.</p>
<h2>Etiology (Causes)</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Neurodegenerative:</strong> Alzheimer's, Parkinson's.</li>
<li><strong>Vascular:</strong> Stroke, small vessel disease.</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic:</strong> Hypothyroidism, B12 deficiency.</li>
<li><strong>Psychiatric:</strong> Major Depression (&quot;Pseudodementia&quot;), Anxiety.</li>
<li><strong>Iatrogenic:</strong> Side effects of medications (e.g., anticholinergics).</li>
</ul>
