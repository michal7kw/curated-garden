<h1>Creativity</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Creativity is the cognitive ability to produce ideas or artifacts that are both novel and valuable. Neuroscience research reveals that creativity is not localized to a single brain region but emerges from the dynamic interaction of three large-scale brain networks: the default mode network (spontaneous idea generation), the executive control network (idea evaluation and refinement), and the salience network (switching between the two). Highly creative individuals show greater functional connectivity between these networks.</p>
<h2>Key Components</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Divergent thinking</strong> — Generating multiple solutions to open-ended problems (PFC, temporal cortex)</li>
<li><strong>Convergent thinking</strong> — Finding the single best solution (dorsolateral PFC)</li>
<li><strong>Remote associations</strong> — Connecting distant concepts (anterior temporal lobe, default mode network)</li>
<li><strong>Insight (&quot;aha!&quot; moments)</strong> — Sudden problem restructuring (right anterior superior temporal gyrus)</li>
<li><strong>Imaginative simulation</strong> — Mental time travel and hypothetical scenarios (hippocampus, default mode network)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Neurotransmitter Modulation</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dopamine</strong> — Inverted-U relationship: moderate levels enhance flexibility, too high or low impairs it</li>
<li><strong>Norepinephrine</strong> — Low tonic levels favor broad attention and creative exploration</li>
<li><strong>Serotonin</strong> — May enhance cognitive flexibility through 5-HT2A receptor activation (links to psychedelic research)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Relevance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>ADHD</strong> — Often associated with enhanced divergent thinking despite executive deficits</li>
<li><strong>Bipolar disorder</strong> — Hypomania linked to increased creative output</li>
<li><strong>Depression</strong> — Rumination can impair creative flexibility</li>
<li><strong>Frontotemporal dementia</strong> — Paradoxical artistic emergence in some variants</li>
<li><strong>Schizotypy</strong> — Trait associated with enhanced remote associations</li>
</ul>
