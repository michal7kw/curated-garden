<h1>Tumor Necrosis Factor Alpha (TNF-α)</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Tumor Necrosis Factor Alpha (TNF-α) is a cytokine produced primarily by activated macrophages, though it can also be produced by T cells, NK cells, neutrophils, mast cells, and eosinophils. It is one of the most potent pro-inflammatory mediators in the body, capable of inducing fever, apoptotic cell death, cachexia, and inflammation.</p>
<p>TNF-α measurement is particularly valuable in monitoring autoimmune conditions where anti-TNF therapies (infliximab, adalimumab, etanercept) are used. It helps guide treatment decisions and assess therapeutic response. Elevated levels are also seen in obesity (adipose tissue is a major source), cardiovascular disease, and chronic infections.</p>
