<h1>Urine NGAL</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Urinary neutrophil gelatinase-associated lipocalin (NGAL), a 25-kDa lipocalin family protein released by injured renal tubular epithelial cells. Rises within 2-4 hours of tubular injury — well before serum creatinine elevation (which lags by 24-72 hours). Particularly useful in:</p>
<ul>
<li>Critical care (post-cardiac surgery AKI risk)</li>
<li>Pediatric AKI assessment</li>
<li>Differentiating intrinsic AKI from prerenal azotemia (NGAL elevated in intrinsic, not prerenal)</li>
</ul>
<p>Available as an immunoassay; less specific than NephroCheck but more widely available. Not yet routine in adult outpatient practice.</p>
