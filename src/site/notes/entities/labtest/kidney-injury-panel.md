<h1>Kidney Injury Panel</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Composite urinary biomarker panel for early acute kidney injury detection. Typical components:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>NGAL</strong> (neutrophil gelatinase-associated lipocalin) — tubular injury marker, rises 2-4h post-insult</li>
<li><strong>KIM-1</strong> (kidney injury molecule-1) — proximal tubular injury, rises 12-24h post-insult, persistent</li>
<li><strong>IL-18</strong> — tubular injury and inflammation, rises 4-6h post-insult</li>
<li><strong>Cystatin C</strong> (urinary or serum) — alternative GFR marker, more sensitive than creatinine for mild reduction</li>
</ul>
<p>Composition varies by laboratory; not a single standardized FDA-cleared assay. Used primarily in:</p>
<ul>
<li>Cardiac surgery (AKI risk after CPB)</li>
<li>Sepsis ICU</li>
<li>Drug-induced nephrotoxicity monitoring (cisplatin, vancomycin, aminoglycosides)</li>
</ul>
<p>Adds value over creatinine when early intervention is feasible (fluid management, nephrotoxin avoidance).</p>
