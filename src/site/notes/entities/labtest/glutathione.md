<h1>Glutathione (Total / Reduced)</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The glutathione test measures levels of glutathione (GSH), the body's most abundant intracellular antioxidant. Glutathione is a tripeptide composed of glutamate, cysteine, and glycine that plays essential roles in Phase II liver detoxification, immune cell function, protein and DNA synthesis, and neutralization of reactive oxygen species.</p>
<p>Glutathione exists in two forms: reduced (GSH, the active antioxidant) and oxidized (GSSG, the inactive disulfide form). The GSH/GSSG ratio reflects the cell's redox status and oxidative stress burden. A healthy ratio is typically &gt;100:1. As oxidative stress increases, more GSH is converted to GSSG, lowering the ratio.</p>
<p>Whole blood or erythrocyte glutathione measurement provides the most clinically meaningful assessment, as &gt;98% of glutathione is intracellular. Plasma glutathione levels are very low and less informative.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5)</p>

<h2>Notes</h2>
<ul>
<li>Whole blood measurement is preferred over plasma (&gt;98% is intracellular)</li>
<li>NAC, liposomal glutathione, and whey protein can raise levels</li>
<li>Acetaminophen (Tylenol) depletes glutathione significantly</li>
<li>Exercise initially depletes but chronically upregulates glutathione</li>
<li>Alcohol, smoking, and chronic stress deplete glutathione</li>
</ul>
