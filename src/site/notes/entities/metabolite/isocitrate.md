<h1>Isocitrate</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Isocitrate is a six-carbon tricarboxylic acid intermediate of the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/citric-acid-cycle/" class="internal-link">Citric Acid Cycle</a>, formed by the reversible isomerization of <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/citrate/" class="internal-link">Citrate</a> by aconitase. Its oxidative decarboxylation by <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/isocitrate-dehydrogenase/" class="internal-link">Isocitrate Dehydrogenase</a> (IDH) is the first CO2-releasing and NADH-generating step of the cycle.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Formation</strong>: Aconitase isomerizes citrate → isocitrate (via cis-aconitate)</li>
<li><strong>Oxidation</strong>: IDH3 (NAD+-dependent, mitochondrial) catalyzes isocitrate → <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/alpha-ketoglutarate/" class="internal-link">Alpha-Ketoglutarate</a> + CO2 + <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/nadh/" class="internal-link">NADH</a></li>
<li><strong>Regulation</strong>: IDH3 is allosterically activated by ADP, Ca2+, and isocitrate itself; inhibited by <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/atp/" class="internal-link">ATP</a> and <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/nadh/" class="internal-link">NADH</a></li>
<li><strong>IDH2 isoform</strong>: Mitochondrial NADP+-dependent IDH2 generates NADPH for antioxidant defense</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li>IDH1/IDH2 gain-of-function mutations (found in gliomas and AML) produce 2-hydroxyglutarate (an oncometabolite) instead of alpha-ketoglutarate</li>
<li>The IDH3 reaction is one of three irreversible steps of the TCA cycle that determine cycle flux</li>
</ul>
