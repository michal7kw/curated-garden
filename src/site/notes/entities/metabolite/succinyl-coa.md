<h1>Succinyl-CoA</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Succinyl-CoA is a four-carbon thioester intermediate of the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/citric-acid-cycle/" class="internal-link">Citric Acid Cycle</a>, formed by the oxidative decarboxylation of <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/alpha-ketoglutarate/" class="internal-link">Alpha-Ketoglutarate</a>. Its high-energy CoA thioester bond drives the only substrate-level phosphorylation in the TCA cycle.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Formation</strong>: Alpha-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase complex catalyzes α-KG + CoA + NAD+ → succinyl-CoA + CO2 + <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/nadh/" class="internal-link">NADH</a></li>
<li><strong>Energy capture</strong>: Succinyl-CoA synthetase cleaves the thioester bond: succinyl-CoA + GDP + Pi → <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/succinate/" class="internal-link">Succinate</a> + CoA + GTP</li>
<li><strong>GTP→ATP</strong>: Nucleoside diphosphate kinase converts GTP to <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/atp/" class="internal-link">ATP</a></li>
<li><strong>Heme precursor</strong>: Succinyl-CoA + glycine → δ-aminolevulinic acid (ALA), the first step of heme biosynthesis</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li>Alpha-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase is sensitive to oxidative stress and is impaired in neurodegenerative diseases</li>
<li>Succinyl-CoA is essential for heme synthesis — deficiency contributes to sideroblastic anemia</li>
<li>Methylmalonyl-CoA (from odd-chain fatty acid and amino acid metabolism) enters the TCA cycle as succinyl-CoA, requiring vitamin B12</li>
</ul>
