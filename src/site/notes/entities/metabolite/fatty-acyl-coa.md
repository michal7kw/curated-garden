<h1>Fatty Acyl-CoA</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Fatty acyl-CoA is the activated (thioester) form of a fatty acid, required for entry into <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/beta-oxidation/" class="internal-link">Beta-Oxidation</a>. It is formed in the cytoplasm by acyl-CoA synthetase and must be transported into the mitochondrial matrix via the carnitine shuttle system (<a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/cpt-i/" class="internal-link">CPT-I</a>, translocase, <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/cpt-ii/" class="internal-link">CPT-II</a>).</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Activation</strong>: Fatty acid + CoA + ATP → fatty acyl-CoA + AMP + PPi (catalyzed by acyl-CoA synthetase)</li>
<li><strong>Transport</strong>: Long-chain acyl-CoAs are converted to <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/acylcarnitine/" class="internal-link">Acylcarnitine</a> by <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/cpt-i/" class="internal-link">CPT-I</a> on the outer mitochondrial membrane</li>
<li><strong>Matrix entry</strong>: Acylcarnitine crosses the inner membrane via translocase, then <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/cpt-ii/" class="internal-link">CPT-II</a> regenerates fatty acyl-CoA in the matrix</li>
<li><strong>Oxidation</strong>: Mitochondrial fatty acyl-CoA undergoes repeated β-oxidation cycles, each removing 2 carbons as <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/acetyl-coa/" class="internal-link">Acetyl-CoA</a> and producing <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/fadh2/" class="internal-link">FADH2</a> + <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/nadh/" class="internal-link">NADH</a></li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li>Malonyl-CoA (from fatty acid synthesis) inhibits <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/cpt-i/" class="internal-link">CPT-I</a>, preventing simultaneous synthesis and oxidation</li>
<li>Carnitine deficiency impairs long-chain fatty acyl-CoA transport, causing energy crisis in heart and muscle</li>
<li>Very long-chain fatty acyl-CoAs (&gt;C22) are processed in peroxisomes rather than mitochondria</li>
</ul>
