<h1>3-Ketoacyl-CoA</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>3-Ketoacyl-CoA is the final intermediate in each <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/beta-oxidation/" class="internal-link">Beta-Oxidation</a> cycle. <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/thiolase/" class="internal-link">Thiolase</a> cleaves it into <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/acetyl-coa/" class="internal-link">Acetyl-CoA</a> (which enters the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/citric-acid-cycle/" class="internal-link">Citric Acid Cycle</a>) and a shortened fatty acyl-CoA that re-enters the next round of beta-oxidation.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Formation</strong>: 3-Hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydrogenase oxidizes <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/3-hydroxyacyl-coa/" class="internal-link">3-Hydroxyacyl-CoA</a> + NAD+ → 3-ketoacyl-CoA + <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/nadh/" class="internal-link">NADH</a></li>
<li><strong>Thiolytic cleavage</strong>: <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/thiolase/" class="internal-link">Thiolase</a> (β-ketothiolase) catalyzes 3-ketoacyl-CoA + CoA → <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/acetyl-coa/" class="internal-link">Acetyl-CoA</a> + acyl-CoA(n-2)</li>
<li><strong>Cycle restart</strong>: The shortened acyl-CoA re-enters the cycle at the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/acyl-coa-dehydrogenase/" class="internal-link">Acyl-CoA Dehydrogenase</a> step</li>
<li><strong>Complete oxidation</strong>: A 16-carbon palmitate produces 8 acetyl-CoA through 7 cycles of beta-oxidation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li>Beta-ketothiolase deficiency causes accumulation of C5 intermediates and episodic ketoacidosis</li>
<li>The acetyl-CoA produced feeds into the TCA cycle or ketogenesis (in liver during fasting)</li>
</ul>
