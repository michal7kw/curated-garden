<h1>Fumarate</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Fumarate (trans-butenedioic acid) is a four-carbon unsaturated dicarboxylic acid that occupies a strategic position in the citric acid cycle (TCA cycle) as the product of succinate dehydrogenase (Complex II) and the substrate for fumarase. In the canonical TCA cycle, succinate is oxidized to fumarate by the FAD-dependent succinate dehydrogenase embedded in the inner mitochondrial membrane, and fumarate is then hydrated by the stereospecific enzyme fumarase to form L-malate. This two-step sequence links the TCA cycle to the electron transport chain, as succinate dehydrogenase is both a TCA cycle enzyme and Complex II of the respiratory chain, feeding electrons directly into the ubiquinone pool.</p>
<p>Beyond its central role in energy metabolism, fumarate has emerged as a critical signaling molecule and oncometabolite. When fumarate accumulates due to loss-of-function mutations in the fumarate hydratase (FH) gene, it acts as a competitive inhibitor of alpha-ketoglutarate-dependent dioxygenases. This inhibition has profound downstream effects: stabilization of hypoxia-inducible factor 1-alpha (HIF-1alpha) through prolyl hydroxylase inhibition creates a pseudohypoxic state that promotes angiogenesis and glycolytic metabolism, while inhibition of histone demethylases (KDMs) and TET DNA demethylases alters epigenetic regulation and gene expression. These effects drive tumor progression in hereditary leiomyomatosis and renal cell cancer (HLRCC syndrome).</p>
<p>Fumarate also participates in amino acid catabolism (the urea cycle and purine nucleotide cycle), where it is generated as a byproduct of argininosuccinate lyase and adenylosuccinate lyase reactions. This links nitrogen metabolism to the TCA cycle through anaplerotic replenishment. The therapeutic agent dimethyl fumarate (Tecfidera), used in multiple sclerosis, exploits fumarate's signaling properties — it activates the Nrf2 antioxidant pathway through Keap1 succination and exerts immunomodulatory effects.</p>
<h2>Role in the Citric Acid Cycle</h2>
<h3>Position in the Cycle</h3>
<p>Fumarate occupies the sixth position in the eight-step TCA cycle:</p>
<p>Succinyl-CoA → Succinate → <strong>Fumarate</strong> → Malate → Oxaloacetate → Citrate → Isocitrate → Alpha-Ketoglutarate → Succinyl-CoA</p>
<h3>Step 6: Succinate → Fumarate</h3>
<p><strong>Enzyme:</strong> Succinate dehydrogenase (SDH, Complex II)<br />
<strong>Cofactor:</strong> FAD (covalently bound)<br />
<strong>Reaction:</strong> Succinate + FAD → Fumarate + FADH2<br />
<strong>Energy yield:</strong> FADH2 feeds directly into the electron transport chain at ubiquinone, yielding approximately 1.5 ATP</p>
<p>SDH is unique among TCA cycle enzymes as it is embedded in the inner mitochondrial membrane and functions simultaneously as Complex II of the electron transport chain. Unlike other TCA cycle enzymes, it does not have a cytosolic isoform.</p>
<h3>Step 7: Fumarate → Malate</h3>
<p><strong>Enzyme:</strong> Fumarase (fumarate hydratase, FH)<br />
<strong>Reaction:</strong> Fumarate + H2O → L-Malate<br />
<strong>Key property:</strong> Fumarase is highly stereospecific — it produces exclusively L-malate from fumarate. The reverse reaction (malate → fumarate) is used in the urea cycle.</p>
<h2>Beyond the TCA Cycle</h2>
<h3>The Urea Cycle Connection</h3>
<p>Fumarate is produced in the cytosol as a byproduct of the urea cycle:</p>
<p>Argininosuccinate → Arginine + Fumarate (catalyzed by argininosuccinate lyase)</p>
<p>This cytosolic fumarate can be converted to malate by cytosolic fumarase and then to oxaloacetate, linking nitrogen excretion to gluconeogenesis and TCA cycle anaplerosis.</p>
<h3>Purine Nucleotide Cycle</h3>
<p>In skeletal muscle, fumarate is generated during the purine nucleotide cycle:</p>
<p>Adenylosuccinate → AMP + Fumarate (catalyzed by adenylosuccinate lyase)</p>
<p>This provides an anaplerotic source of TCA cycle intermediates during intense exercise when amino acid transamination cannot keep pace with TCA cycle demands.</p>
<h3>Protein Succination</h3>
<p>Accumulated fumarate reacts non-enzymatically with cysteine thiol groups on proteins to form S-(2-succinyl)cysteine (2SC), a post-translational modification called succination:</p>
<p>Fumarate + Protein-SH → Protein-S-(2-succinyl)cysteine</p>
<p>Key succination targets include:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Keap1:</strong> Succination releases Nrf2, activating antioxidant gene expression (therapeutic mechanism of dimethyl fumarate)</li>
<li><strong>GapDH:</strong> Succination impairs glycolytic flux, contributing to the Warburg-like metabolism in FH-deficient tumors</li>
<li><strong>Mitochondrial proteins:</strong> Succination disrupts iron-sulfur cluster proteins and aconitase activity</li>
</ul>
<h3>Oncometabolite Activity (FH Deficiency)</h3>
<p>Loss of fumarate hydratase (FH) — whether through germline mutation (HLRCC syndrome) or somatic inactivation — causes fumarate accumulation with several oncogenic consequences:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Pseudohypoxia:</strong> Fumarate inhibits prolyl hydroxylases (PHDs), preventing HIF-1alpha degradation. Stabilized HIF-1alpha activates VEGF, GLUT1, and glycolytic enzymes, promoting angiogenesis and the Warburg effect.</li>
<li><strong>Epigenetic dysregulation:</strong> Fumarate inhibits histone demethylases (KDM4A/B) and TET DNA demethylases, causing hypermethylation and silencing of tumor suppressor genes.</li>
<li><strong>EMT activation:</strong> Succination of regulatory proteins promotes epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition, enhancing metastatic potential.</li>
<li><strong>Genomic instability:</strong> Impaired DNA repair through inhibition of alpha-KG-dependent repair enzymes (ALKBH family).</li>
</ol>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<h3>HLRCC Syndrome (Hereditary Leiomyomatosis and Renal Cell Cancer)</h3>
<p>Autosomal dominant disorder caused by germline FH mutations:</p>
<ul>
<li>Cutaneous and uterine leiomyomas (benign smooth muscle tumors)</li>
<li>Type 2 papillary renal cell carcinoma (aggressive, early metastasis)</li>
<li>Fumarate accumulation detectable in tumor tissue as 2SC immunostaining (diagnostic marker)</li>
<li>Management: surveillance, early surgical intervention for renal tumors</li>
</ul>
<h3>Dimethyl Fumarate in Multiple Sclerosis</h3>
<p>DMF (Tecfidera, approved 2013) is an FDA-approved first-line treatment for relapsing-remitting MS:</p>
<ul>
<li>Rapidly hydrolyzed to monomethyl fumarate (MMF), the active metabolite</li>
<li>Activates Nrf2 antioxidant pathway via Keap1 succination</li>
<li>Shifts immune response from pro-inflammatory (Th1/Th17) to anti-inflammatory (Th2)</li>
<li>Reduces relapse rate by approximately 50% in clinical trials</li>
<li>Side effects: flushing, gastrointestinal distress, lymphopenia</li>
</ul>
<h3>SDH-Related Paragangliomas</h3>
<p>While SDH mutations primarily cause succinate accumulation, impaired SDH activity also elevates fumarate, contributing to similar oncometabolite effects through dioxygenase inhibition and HIF stabilization.</p>
<h3>Fumaric Acid Esters in Psoriasis</h3>
<p>Fumaric acid esters (Fumaderm) have been used in Germany for psoriasis treatment since 1994:</p>
<ul>
<li>Anti-inflammatory effects through NF-kB pathway modulation</li>
<li>Reduces keratinocyte proliferation and immune cell infiltration</li>
<li>Mechanism overlaps with MS treatment (Nrf2 activation, immune modulation)</li>
</ul>
