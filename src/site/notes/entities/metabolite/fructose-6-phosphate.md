<h1>Fructose-6-Phosphate</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Fructose-6-phosphate is a glycolytic intermediate formed by the reversible isomerization of <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/glucose-6-phosphate/" class="internal-link">Glucose-6-Phosphate</a> by phosphoglucose isomerase. It serves as the substrate for <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/phosphofructokinase/" class="internal-link">Phosphofructokinase</a> (PFK-1), the committed and rate-limiting step of <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/glycolysis/" class="internal-link">Glycolysis</a>.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>The conversion of fructose-6-phosphate to <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/fructose-16-bisphosphate/" class="internal-link">Fructose-1,6-Bisphosphate</a> by PFK-1 is the primary regulatory checkpoint of glycolysis. This step is:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Activated by</strong>: AMP, ADP, fructose-2,6-bisphosphate, Pi</li>
<li><strong>Inhibited by</strong>: <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/atp/" class="internal-link">ATP</a>, <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/citrate/" class="internal-link">Citrate</a>, low pH</li>
<li>The allosteric regulation by ATP/AMP makes this step the key sensor of cellular energy status, linking glycolytic flux to <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/ampk-pathway/" class="internal-link">AMPK Pathway</a> signaling</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<p>Fructose-6-phosphate levels reflect the balance between glucose entry into glycolysis versus the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/pentose-phosphate-pathway/" class="internal-link">Pentose Phosphate Pathway</a>. Elevated levels may indicate PFK deficiency (Tarui disease/glycogen storage disease VII).</p>
