<h1>Fructose-1,6-Bisphosphate</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate (F1,6BP) is the product of the committed step of <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/glycolysis/" class="internal-link">Glycolysis</a>, formed when <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/phosphofructokinase/" class="internal-link">Phosphofructokinase</a> phosphorylates <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/fructose-6-phosphate/" class="internal-link">Fructose-6-Phosphate</a> using <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/atp/" class="internal-link">ATP</a>. It marks the point of no return in glycolysis — once formed, the carbon is committed to the glycolytic pathway.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Formation</strong>: PFK-1 catalyzes F6P + ATP → F1,6BP + ADP (irreversible under physiological conditions)</li>
<li><strong>Cleavage</strong>: Aldolase splits F1,6BP into <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate/" class="internal-link">Glyceraldehyde-3-Phosphate</a> (G3P) and dihydroxyacetone phosphate (DHAP)</li>
<li><strong>Feedforward activation</strong>: F1,6BP allosterically activates <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/protein/pyruvate-kinase/" class="internal-link">Pyruvate Kinase</a>, linking upper and lower glycolysis</li>
<li><strong>Gluconeogenesis reversal</strong>: Fructose-1,6-bisphosphatase converts F1,6BP back to F6P in <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/gluconeogenesis/" class="internal-link">Gluconeogenesis</a></li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<p>The PFK reaction consuming ATP to produce F1,6BP is a net energy investment. Deficiency in aldolase (aldolase deficiency) can cause hereditary fructose intolerance and hemolytic anemia.</p>
