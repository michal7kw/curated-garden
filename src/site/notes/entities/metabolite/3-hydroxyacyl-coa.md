<h1>3-Hydroxyacyl-CoA</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>L-3-Hydroxyacyl-CoA is the second intermediate in the <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/pathway/beta-oxidation/" class="internal-link">Beta-Oxidation</a> spiral, formed by hydration of <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/enoyl-coa/" class="internal-link">Enoyl-CoA</a>. Its oxidation by 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydrogenase produces <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/nadh/" class="internal-link">NADH</a>, contributing to the energy yield of each beta-oxidation cycle.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Formation</strong>: Enoyl-CoA hydratase catalyzes enoyl-CoA + H2O → L-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA</li>
<li><strong>Oxidation</strong>: 3-Hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydrogenase oxidizes the hydroxyl group: L-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA + NAD+ → <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/3-ketoacyl-coa/" class="internal-link">3-Ketoacyl-CoA</a> + <a href="/garden/michal.k7w@gmail.com/metabolite/nadh/" class="internal-link">NADH</a></li>
<li><strong>Part of trifunctional protein</strong>: In mitochondria, the hydratase and dehydrogenase activities for long-chain substrates reside on the mitochondrial trifunctional protein (MTP)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li>MTP deficiency (LCHAD deficiency) is a serious fatty acid oxidation disorder causing hypoketotic hypoglycemia, cardiomyopathy, and acute liver disease</li>
<li>3-Hydroxyacyl-CoA accumulation is diagnostic for specific beta-oxidation disorders</li>
</ul>
